# USAGE GUIDE: How to Use the CGRA Syllabus & Dossier System

## Quick Start

The CGRA Syllabus consists of two complementary formats:

- **SYLLABUS_COURTE.pdf** (20 pages): Quick reference guide for experienced staff
- **SYLLABUS_COMPLETE.pdf** (80 pages): Comprehensive training manual for detailed learning
- **dossiers/** (6 folders): Organized reference materials for specific tasks

**New users should start here → Go to "Path for New Users" below**

**Experienced staff can jump to → "Quick Reference by Task" section**

---

## Understanding the System Architecture

### The Three Layers

```
LAYER 1: SYLLABUS_COURTE (20p)
├─ Essential concepts
├─ Key procedures
├─ Quick answers
└─ For: Quick lookups, experienced users

LAYER 2: DOSSIERS (30+ files in 6 folders)
├─ Detailed templates
├─ Checklists
├─ Case examples
├─ Procedures with timelines
└─ For: Specific tasks, reference during work

LAYER 3: SYLLABUS_COMPLETE (80p)
├─ Complete training material
├─ All context and background
├─ Detailed explanations
├─ Full case studies
└─ For: Initial training, understanding framework
```

### How They Work Together

- **SYLLABUS_COURTE** answers the question: "What do I do?"
- **DOSSIERS** answer: "How exactly do I do this?"
- **SYLLABUS_COMPLETE** explains: "Why do I do this this way?"

## Recommended Reading Paths

### Path 1: New Users (First 1-2 Weeks)

**Week 1 - Foundation**

1. Read: **SYLLABUS_COURTE.pdf** (20 pages - 3 hours)
   - Get overview of entire system
   - Understand legal framework
   - See major procedures

2. Study: **dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/** (all files)
   - Checklist_Tuteur_Detailed.md (obligations and timeline)
   - Obligations_Legales_Detailed.md (legal framework)
   - Obligations_Financieres.md (insurance, taxes)
   - Droits_MENA.md (child's rights - your obligations)
   - README.md (folder overview)

3. Review: **dossiers/05_Ressources/** (emergency contacts section)
   - Contacts_Urgence.md (bookmark for immediate access)
   - Know where to call for any emergency

**Week 2 - Application**

4. Study: **dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/** (all files)
   - Procedure_Asile_Complete.md
   - Procedure_Famille.md (if working with family contact)
   - Procedure_Signalement.md (mandatory reporting)
   - Other procedures relevant to your role

5. Review: **dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/** (all case files)
   - Read each case
   - Complete Case_Analysis_Worksheet.md for 2-3 cases
   - Discuss with supervisor

6. Bookmark: **dossiers/06_Templates_Lettres/** (for future use)
   - Review available templates
   - Know how to access them

**By End of Week 2**: You should be able to:
- Explain legal obligations
- Name emergency contacts
- Describe major procedures
- Discuss real cases
- Know where to find specific information

---

### Path 2: Experienced Users (Initial Orientation)

1. **Skim** SYLLABUS_COURTE.pdf (15 minutes)
   - Identify what's new or different
   - Check any updated procedures

2. **Quick Review** dossiers/ folder structure (10 minutes)
   - Understand organization
   - Bookmark essential sections

3. **Jump to Specific Tasks** (as needed)
   - Use "Quick Reference by Task" below

**Ongoing**: Refer back to specific dossiers as situations arise

---

### Path 3: Managers/Supervisors (First Day)

1. **Priority Read**: SYLLABUS_COURTE.pdf (20 minutes)
   - Understand framework
   - Identify compliance requirements

2. **Study**: 
   - dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/ (Obligations_Legales_Detailed.md)
   - dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/ (all procedures)
   - Understanding reporting requirements and timelines

3. **Know**: Emergency contacts and escalation procedures
   - dossiers/05_Ressources/Contacts_Urgence.md

4. **Plan**: Using dossiers for staff training
   - dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/ for case discussions
   - Case_Analysis_Worksheet.md for supervision

**Ongoing**: Use for staff development and quality assurance

---

### Path 4: Training Instructors (Program Design)

1. **Complete Study**: SYLLABUS_COMPLETE.pdf (full read, 8-10 hours)
   - Understand entire framework
   - See all context and reasoning

2. **Explore**: All dossiers (2-3 hours)
   - Understand how materials connect
   - Plan how to use each in training

3. **Review**: IMPLEMENTATION_GUIDE.md
   - 4-week curriculum design
   - Assessment rubrics
   - Discussion questions

4. **Plan**: Training program using materials
   - Use dossiers as training modules
   - Adapt Case_Analysis_Worksheet for exercises
   - Structure according to 4-week model

## Quick Reference by Task

### "I need to help a child who has just arrived"

**Start with these documents (in order)**:
1. dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/Checklist_Tuteur_Detailed.md - Daily checklist
2. dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/CASE_001_unaccompanied.md - Real example
3. dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/Procedure_Accueil.md (if exists) or Procedure_Asile_Complete.md
4. dossiers/05_Ressources/Organisations.md - Available services

**Key Points**: 
- Safety assessment comes first
- Multiple organizations may be involved
- Asylum procedure may not start immediately
- Document everything

---

### "I need to prepare an asylum application"

**Start with these documents (in order)**:
1. dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/Procedure_Asile_Complete.md - Full procedure
2. dossiers/02_Formulaires_CGRA/Demande_Asile.md - Form guidance
3. dossiers/02_Formulaires_CGRA/Demande_Asile_Guide.md (if exists) - Filled example
4. dossiers/06_Templates_Lettres/Lettre_Autorite.md - Communications template

**Key Points**:
- Timeline matters (strict deadlines)
- Documentation is critical
- CGRA deadlines are mandatory
- Get legal help if possible

---

### "The child needs medical attention"

**Start with these documents (in order)**:
1. dossiers/05_Ressources/Contacts_Urgence.md - Emergency contacts (if urgent)
2. dossiers/05_Ressources/Centres.md - Healthcare facility list
3. dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/Obligations_Legales_Detailed.md - Your responsibility
4. dossiers/06_Templates_Lettres/Lettre_Sante.md (if exists) - Health communication

**Key Points**:
- Child's wellbeing is primary
- Don't wait for bureaucracy (get medical help)
- Document medical care received
- Follow up on treatment

---

### "The child is missing/may be at risk"

**START HERE IMMEDIATELY** (no reading ahead):
1. Call: Contact information in dossiers/05_Ressources/Contacts_Urgence.md
   - Police: 17 (or 112 emergency)
   - Child protection: [Regional authority number]
   - Your supervisor: [Supervisor contact]

**THEN, after emergency reported**:
2. dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/Procedure_Disparition.md (if exists)
3. dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/Obligations_Legales_Detailed.md - What you must do
4. dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/CASE_004_runaway.md - How similar situations handled

**Key Points**:
- Call first, read later
- Document everything
- Cooperate fully with authorities
- Child safety is only consideration

---

### "I suspect the child is being abused"

**START IMMEDIATELY** (no reading ahead):
1. Call: Contact in dossiers/05_Ressources/Contacts_Urgence.md
   - Child protection authority (SAMU: 15, Police: 17, or local authority)
   - Your supervisor
   - Do NOT contact the family or suspected abuser

**THEN, after reporting**:
2. dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/Procedure_Signalement.md - Reporting procedure
3. dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/Obligations_Legales_Detailed.md - Your legal obligation
4. dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/ - Relevant case for your situation

**Key Points**:
- Reasonable suspicion is enough (no proof needed)
- Mandatory reporting is legal duty
- You are protected from retaliation
- Keep records of your report

---

### "The family is requesting family contact"

**Start with these documents (in order)**:
1. dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/Procedure_Famille.md - Family procedure
2. dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/CASE_002_family_conflict.md - Real example
3. dossiers/06_Templates_Lettres/Lettre_Famille.md - Family communication
4. dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/Obligations_Legales.md - Legal framework

**Key Points**:
- Child's safety is primary
- Some contact may be essential
- Some contact may be harmful
- Assessment needed before proceeding

---

### "I need to help with school enrollment"

**Start with these documents (in order)**:
1. dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/ - School procedure (if exists)
2. dossiers/05_Ressources/Organisations.md - School contact information
3. dossiers/06_Templates_Lettres/ - School communication templates
4. dossiers/02_Formulaires_CGRA/ - Required forms (if applicable)

**Key Points**:
- Age-appropriate placement
- Language support assessment
- Documentation (birth cert, vaccination, etc.)
- Early enrollment (don't wait)

---

### "I need to write an official letter"

**Start here**:
1. dossiers/06_Templates_Lettres/README.md - Overview of available templates
2. Find specific template:
   - Lettre_Autorite.md (to government)
   - Lettre_Avocat.md (to lawyer)
   - Lettre_Sante.md (to healthcare)
   - Lettre_Famille.md (to family)
   - Lettre_Ecole.md (to school)

3. Adapt template for your situation
4. Have supervisor review before sending

**Key Points**:
- Professional tone
- Clear, specific requests
- Keep copies
- Follow up in writing

---

### "I need to understand a specific child's case"

**Start here**:
1. dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/CASE_FINDER.md (or INDEX_BY_*) - Find similar cases
2. Read the matching case file
3. Complete dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/Case_Analysis_Worksheet.md for this child's situation
4. Discuss with supervisor in next meeting

**Key Points**:
- Real cases build understanding
- Worksheet helps organize thinking
- Supervisor discussion adds expertise
- Document your analysis

## Tips for Effective Use

### Organization Tips
- Keep a folder with printed essentials at your desk
- Bookmark digital folders in your browser
- Create shortcuts to frequently used files
- Organize by frequency of use (daily, weekly, as-needed)

### Learning Tips
- Don't try to read everything at once
- Follow the recommended reading path for your role
- Read with your supervisor
- Practice with Case_Analysis_Worksheet
- Discuss cases in team meetings

### Reference Tips
- Use FINDER documents to locate what you need
- Cross-references at end of each document show connections
- Index documents help find by type/domain/age
- README in each folder explains what's there

### Compliance Tips
- Checklist items align with legal obligations
- Procedures include mandatory steps
- Templates follow legal/professional standards
- Keep copies of all actions taken
- Document decisions and reasoning
