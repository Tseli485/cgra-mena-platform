## CHAPTER 11: QUICK REFERENCE GUIDES

### Decision Tree 1: Safety Assessment

**START: Imminent Danger?**
- YES → Emergency response, immediate removal if necessary, law enforcement involved
- NO → Continue to next assessment

**Physical Abuse or Severe Neglect Present?**
- YES → Serious safety concern, protective measures required
- NO → Continue to next assessment

**Sexual Abuse or Exploitation Risk?**
- YES → Specialized assessment, protective separation if needed
- NO → Continue to next assessment

**Parental Capacity to Protect?**
- ADEQUATE → Services to support; home-based plan
- INADEQUATE → More intensive intervention; possible removal

**Imminent Danger Despite Services?**
- YES → Removal and placement; family reunification planning
- NO → Enhanced services; continued monitoring

---

### Decision Tree 2: Educational Placement

**Does child have documented disability?**
- YES → Proceed to special education evaluation
- NO → Universal screening for learning and developmental concerns

**Meets special education criteria?**
- YES → Develop Individualized Education Plan (IEP)
- NO → Consider 504 plan or other accommodations

**Current placement appropriate?**
- YES → Continue with monitoring and adjustment
- NO → Evaluate alternative placement options

**What level of support is needed?**
- Minimal support → General education with accommodations
- Moderate support → Special education resource services
- Intensive support → Special education classroom/program
- Maximum support → Specialized school or alternative program

---

### Decision Tree 3: Mental Health & Trauma Response

**Active suicidality or self-harm risk?**
- YES → Safety assessment, possible psychiatric hold, emergency services
- NO → Continue assessment

**Recent trauma or abuse disclosure?**
- YES → Trauma-focused assessment, safety planning, trauma-informed services
- NO → Continue assessment

**Mental health diagnosis present?**
- YES → Psychiatric evaluation, medication consideration if appropriate, therapy
- NO → Screening and assessment for symptoms

**Substance use present?**
- YES → Substance use assessment, prevention/treatment services
- NO → Prevention education, screening continued

**Adequate support system?**
- YES → Community-based services with family/support involvement
- NO → Enhanced services, possible more intensive placement

## CHAPTER 12: IMPLEMENTATION GUIDANCE

### Starting Implementation in Your Organization

1. **Assessment & Readiness**
   - Assess current capacity, gaps, and needs
   - Identify key stakeholders and champions
   - Review existing policies and procedures
   - Conduct staff training needs assessment

2. **Planning**
   - Establish implementation team
   - Set realistic timelines and milestones
   - Identify resources needed
   - Create implementation plan with clear roles

3. **Staff Development**
   - Provide initial training on frameworks and approaches
   - Ongoing supervision and coaching
   - Case consultation and peer learning
   - Regular competency assessment

4. **Quality Assurance**
   - Regular case reviews
   - Outcome monitoring
   - Family/youth feedback collection
   - Continuous improvement

5. **Integration With Systems**
   - Coordination protocols with schools
   - Mental health provider partnerships
   - Law enforcement communication
   - Community resource mapping
