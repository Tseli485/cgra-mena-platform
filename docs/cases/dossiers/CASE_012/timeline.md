# Timeline - Additional Complex Case - Legal, Employment & Family Intersections


**2010 (Birth)**
- Bashir born to Amal and Ibrahim
- Initial years: household relatively stable; father present in early childhood

**2012-2018 (Age 2-8)**
- Father Ibrahim's involvement declines as marriage deteriorates
- Amal works as cleaner; extended family provides some support
- Bashir attends primary school; performance adequate
- Age 6: Father remarries; contact with Bashir becomes minimal
- Karim (mother's second husband) joins household; initially relationship neutral

**2018-2020 (Age 8-10)**
- Karim's employment becomes irregular
- Two half-siblings born (ages 6 and 4 by 2025)
- Household finances increasingly stressed
- Bashir's school attendance remains consistent
- Extended family support begins to diminish

**2021-2022 (Age 11-12)**
- Amal loses cleaning job; remains unemployed for extended period
- Karim's income insufficient for household; tensions increase
- First signs of family stress: increased marital conflict
- Karim's behavior becomes increasingly controlling and aggressive
- Bashir's school attendance begins to drop (89% to 76%)

**2023 (Age 13)**
- Household falls into poverty; basic needs not consistently met
- Amal desperately seeks work; takes occasional informal jobs
- Bashir completely leaves school; no longer attends
- Mustafa (distant relative) offers Bashir work in auto repair shop
- Bashir begins working 10-12 hours daily; earns $3-5 per day
- Work involves dangerous machinery, toxic chemicals, no safety equipment
- Karim becomes verbally abusive; first incident of physical punishment

**2024 (Age 14)**
- Bashir continues informal labor; income becomes critical to family survival
- Work conditions remain hazardous; safety concerns raised periodically by Mustafa's employees (but not acted upon)
- Karim's abuse escalates; physical punishment becomes regular occurrence
- Amal attempts to intervene; relationship becomes more strained
- Bashir shows signs of depression and anxiety
- August: Bashir sustains serious hand injury (laceration from machinery)
- Injury initially treated with home remedies; no medical care accessed
- Infection develops (within 1 week); fever, pus, swelling evident
- Karim blames Bashir; physical argument ensues; Karim hits Bashir
- Systemic infection apparent; Bashir becomes seriously ill (fever, weakness, delirium)

**2025 (January)**
- Neighbor notices Bashir's severe injury, illness, and deteriorating condition
- Neighbor contacts local police; investigation initiated
- Medical assessment reveals severe hand infection; wound culture confirms pathogenic bacteria
- Police interview reveals child labor, abuse, medical neglect
- Bashir referred to child protection; Mme. Farah Hassan assigned

**2025 (January-February)**
- Emergency medical care: hand wound assessment, infection treatment, antibiotics
- Hand surgery consultation; assessment of potential permanent damage
- Psychological assessment: trauma symptoms, depression, identity disruption
- Educational assessment: Bashir 3-4 grades behind; literacy and numeracy gaps significant
- Family assessment: abuse, violence, economic crisis, parenting capacity concerns
- Legal investigation: child labor, abuse, stepfather accountability
- Safety assessment: home environment and child's protection needs

**2025 (March-April)**
- Hand infection treatment continues; gradual healing; some loss of fine motor control evident
- Medical follow-up: wound monitoring, physical therapy assessment
- Sheltering decision: initial removal from home for safety; placed in child protection facility
- Psychological counseling begins; trauma processing and emotional support
- Educational program initiation: non-formal education; basic literacy and numeracy focus
- Family mediation initiated; individual counseling for mother Amal
- Legal consultation regarding child labor case and abuse investigation
- Police investigation continues; evidence gathered for prosecution

**2025 (May-June)**
- Hand healing progressing; residual scarring and slight loss of dexterity; long-term prognosis improving
- Medical rehabilitation: occupational therapy for hand function
- Psychological support intensifying; addressing depression and trauma
- Educational progress beginning; Bashir engaging with learning; catch-up underway
- Family assessment determines mother's capacity for protecting Bashir
- Karim's whereabouts and legal status assessed (investigation ongoing)
- Reunification planning begins; conditions for safe return established
- Economic assessment: family's income options without child labor explored

**2025 (July-August)**
- Criminal charges filed against Karim for abuse and against Mustafa for child labor violations
- Karim arrested; removed from household
- Bashir's sheltering status reviewed; mother's home assessed for safety
- Mother Amal enrolled in psychoeducation and parenting support
- Economic support initiated: temporary cash assistance
- Bashir transitions to home-based care with frequent monitoring
- School re-enrollment planned (vocational track given age and gaps)

**2025 (September-October)**
- Bashir re-enrolled in second-chance education program (vocational track)
- School attendance improving; engagement with peers beginning
- Hand function improving with rehabilitation; returning to near-normal capability
- Psychological symptoms improving; depression reducing; engagement with life increasing
- Family relationships stabilizing; Karim's absence allows household stress to decrease
- Economic support continuing; mother exploring legitimate work opportunities

**2025 (November-December)**
- Bashir completes first semester of vocational program; passes courses
- Criminal case against Karim proceeds through courts (slow process)
- Hand function nearly fully recovered; minor residual scarring
- Psychological recovery progressing; peer relationships developing
- Mother's work situation stabilizing; found part-time employment
- Family's economic situation improving; crisis phase ending
- Monitoring continuing; protective factors strengthening

**2026 (January)**
- One year from case opening; case review and transition planning
- Bashir continuing vocational education; on track for completion
- Hand fully functional; occupational therapy completed
- Psychological wellbeing improving; resilience evident
- Family relationships stable; safety concerns addressed
- Criminal proceedings ongoing but not disrupting family stability
- Transition planning: from intensive intervention to monitoring phase
- Evaluation of case progress and outcomes
