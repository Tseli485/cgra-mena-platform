# CYCLE-VIE Phase 2: Ages 2-6 Years
## Kindergarten Age & Social Integration

**Duration:** 24-72 months from arrival  
**Phase Type:** Educational Integration & Social Development  
**Key Focus:** Kindergarten enrollment, language development, social integration, and preparation for primary education

---

## 1. Story & Overview

The 2-6 years phase represents a transformative period marked by kindergarten enrollment, accelerated language development, and social integration into the broader community. This phase encompasses:

### Language Assessment & Training (Months 24-36)
- **Initial Language Assessment:** Evaluation of current language abilities in native language and host country language
- **Multilingual Development Plan:** Structured approach to develop host language while maintaining cultural identity
- **Speech & Language Services:** Professional assessment and intervention for language delays or speech issues
- **Immersion into Host Language Kindergarten:** Enrollment in French-language kindergarten programs
- **Family Language Maintenance:** Strategies for maintaining connection to family language and culture

### Kindergarten Enrollment (Months 24-48)
- **School Selection & Registration:** Choice of appropriate kindergarten program and official enrollment
- **School Readiness Assessment:** Evaluation of social, emotional, and physical readiness for school
- **Admission Requirements Completion:** Documentation, health compliance, emergency contacts
- **Transition Support:** Gradual introduction to school environment and routines
- **School-Home Collaboration:** Regular communication between tuteur, school, and family

### Social Integration & Peer Development (Months 36-72)
- **Peer Relationship Development:** Facilitation of friendships and social interaction with classmates
- **Structured Activities:** Participation in school activities, clubs, and community programs
- **Behavioral Development:** Introduction of school rules, peer conflicts, social expectations
- **Cultural Adaptation:** Support for integrating into host culture while maintaining identity
- **Family Contact & Stability:** Ongoing management of family connections and relationship development

### Health Monitoring & Special Needs Support (Ongoing)
- **Annual Health Examinations:** School-required medical evaluations
- **Developmental Screening:** Assessment for learning disabilities, behavioral concerns, developmental delays
- **Special Education Identification:** Early identification and support for children with special needs
- **Psychological Support:** Counseling and mental health services for adaptation challenges
- **Preventive Healthcare:** Continued vaccination, dental care, and health promotion

## 2. Legal Obligations for Tuteur (18+ Required Actions)

### Educational Documentation & Compliance (Actions 1-5)
1. **Kindergarten Registration & Enrollment**
   - Research and select appropriate kindergarten program (age 2-4 typically)
   - Complete school enrollment application and forms
   - Submit required documents to school (proof of residence, identity, vaccination records)
   - Attend school orientation and information sessions
   - Sign school contracts and parent agreements

2. **Language Assessment Documentation**
   - Obtain professional language assessment in native language
   - Document current French/host language proficiency level
   - Create individual language development plan with speech therapist
   - Maintain records of language testing and progression
   - Document any language delays or speech concerns requiring intervention

3. **School Health Compliance**
   - Ensure all vaccinations current per school requirements
   - Obtain school health examination within required timeframe
   - Submit vaccination proof and health records to school
   - Arrange for any required health interventions before school entry
   - Maintain updated health records with school and family file

4. **School Documentation Files**
   - Create and maintain individual school file with all enrollment documents
   - Keep copies of report cards, assessment results, and school communications
   - Document any incidents, behavioral concerns, or special accommodations
   - Maintain emergency contact and parental consent forms
   - Update school with any changes in family situation or contact information

5. **Special Education Evaluation (If Applicable)**
   - Request special education evaluation if developmental delays suspected
   - Obtain comprehensive psycho-educational assessment if needed
   - Document any identified learning disabilities or special needs
   - Develop individualized education plan (IEP) if applicable
   - Coordinate with school special education services and support staff

### Family Contact & Cultural Maintenance (Actions 6-8)
6. **Family Communication Facilitation**
   - Establish and maintain regular contact protocol with located family members
   - Facilitate phone/video calls with family at appropriate frequency
   - Translate family messages and correspondence as needed
   - Document family communications and any relevant developments
   - Assess impact of family contact on child's emotional wellbeing

7. **Family Contact Center Services**
   - Register child with family contact center if cross-border family relations
   - Arrange supervised visits or video contacts through official services
   - Maintain documentation of all family contact sessions
   - Follow family contact center protocols and requirements
   - Address any safety or welfare concerns identified during contact

8. **Cultural Identity & Heritage Maintenance**
   - Ensure access to cultural events, celebrations, and community
   - Maintain connection to native language through family and cultural programs
   - Support child's understanding of cultural background and heritage
   - Facilitate ethnic or cultural community connections if available
   - Balance cultural maintenance with integration into host country

### Health & Development Monitoring (Actions 9-12)
9. **Annual Health Examinations**
   - Schedule and attend annual pediatric check-ups (minimum once yearly)
   - Ensure school-required health examinations are completed
   - Monitor growth and development against age-appropriate standards
   - Address any health concerns identified during examinations
   - Maintain updated health records and vaccination certifications

10. **Developmental Screening & Assessment**
    - Ensure age-appropriate developmental screening is conducted
    - Request early identification assessment if developmental delays observed
    - Document results of developmental, language, or behavioral screening
    - Follow up on any recommended specialist evaluations
    - Implement recommended interventions or therapy programs

11. **Mental Health & Psychological Support**
    - Assess child's emotional adjustment to school and new environment
    - Seek psychological evaluation if behavioral or emotional concerns
    - Arrange counseling or therapy services if recommended
    - Monitor for signs of trauma, anxiety, or behavioral issues
    - Coordinate mental health services with school and family physician

12. **Special Healthcare Needs Management**
    - Identify and document any chronic medical conditions
    - Ensure appropriate medication management and prescriptions
    - Coordinate specialist care if medical conditions identified
    - Maintain emergency health plans and medication access at school
    - Document all medical appointments and treatment outcomes

### Care Plan & Legal Status (Actions 13-15)
13. **Individual Development Plan Updates**
    - Review and update care plan annually or as needed
    - Set educational and developmental goals for school year
    - Document progress toward language proficiency
    - Assess social integration and peer relationship development
    - Plan for next phase transition (primary school preparation)

14. **Legal Status & Guardianship Maintenance**
    - Ensure guardianship documentation remains current and valid
    - Update child's legal status documentation annually
    - Maintain proof of legal residence and identity
    - Renew identification documents as they expire
    - Follow any required legal reporting or compliance procedures

15. **Financial Account & Benefits Management**
    - Maintain transparent accounting of child's benefits and allowances
    - Update benefit applications as child ages (some change at age thresholds)
    - Document all educational and developmental expenses
    - Ensure separate financial management from tuteur personal funds
    - Prepare annual financial reports for oversight authorities

### Welfare Monitoring & Reporting (Actions 16-18)
16. **School Welfare Monitoring**
    - Maintain regular contact with school staff (monthly minimum)
    - Attend parent-teacher conferences and progress meetings
    - Monitor academic progress, behavior, and social development
    - Address any concerns communicated by school immediately
    - Document school observations and communications in case file

17. **Facility & Home Environment Visits**
    - Conduct bi-monthly welfare visits to child's living facility
    - Observe child's emotional state, physical health, and behavior
    - Assess quality of care and living environment conditions
    - Document observations and any concerns requiring attention
    - Address facility concerns with management or authorities as needed

18. **Quarterly Progress Reports & Case Reviews**
    - Prepare comprehensive quarterly progress reports
    - Include educational achievements, language development, social progress
    - Document any health concerns or specialist recommendations
    - Conduct multi-disciplinary team meetings semi-annually
    - Update case file with current status and plan adjustments

## 6. Age-Specific Phases Within 2-6 Years

### Phase 2.1: Early Kindergarten Enrollment (2-3 Years)
**Focus:** Language Assessment, School Readiness, Initial Enrollment

**Key Activities:**
- Language development assessment and intervention planning
- School readiness evaluation and preparation
- Selection of appropriate kindergarten program
- Initial enrollment and registration
- Transition to school environment
- Family contact maintenance and identity building

**Developmental Characteristics:**
- Rapid language development (50+ to 250+ words)
- Emerging social awareness and peer interaction
- Increased independence and self-assertion
- Motor skill development (running, climbing, coordination)
- Play becoming more purposeful and imaginative
- Emerging behavioral management needs
- Recognition of self and basic emotions

**School Readiness Areas:**
- Language comprehension and expression
- Social skills and cooperation ability
- Emotional regulation and impulse control
- Motor skills (fine and gross)
- Attention span and ability to follow instructions
- Independence in basic self-care

**Care Priorities:**
- Language stimulation in both native and host languages
- Social interaction and peer exposure
- Preparation for school structure and routines
- Behavioral boundary setting and expectations
- Continued health monitoring and preventive care
- Cultural and family connection maintenance

**Tuteur Actions:**
- Conduct comprehensive language assessment
- Research and select kindergarten program
- Complete school enrollment and documentation
- Arrange language instruction if needed
- Establish school communication and monitoring
- Document language and developmental progress
- Update care plan for school transition

**Expected Outcomes by Month 36 (Age 3):**
- Child enrolled in kindergarten program
- Language development progressing in host language
- School readiness skills emerging
- Comfortable in school routine
- Peer interaction beginning
- Family connections maintained
- Individual development plan updated for school phase

### Phase 2.2: Language Assessment & Training (2-4 Years)
**Focus:** Language Proficiency Development, Speech Services, Multilingual Support

**Key Activities:**
- Initial and ongoing language assessment
- Speech and language therapy if indicated
- Structured language instruction and support
- Immersion into host language kindergarten
- Maintenance of native language with family
- Assessment of language-based learning concerns
- Collaboration with speech professionals

**Language Development Milestones:**
- Age 2-3: 50-300 word vocabulary, two-word phrases, 50% intelligible to strangers
- Age 3-4: 300-1,500 word vocabulary, simple sentences, mostly intelligible to strangers
- Age 4-6: 1,500+ word vocabulary, complex sentences, fully intelligible to strangers

**Speech & Language Services:**
- Professional assessment of current proficiency
- Identification of speech or language delays
- Individual treatment plans if indicated
- Frequency of therapy (typically 1-2x weekly if needed)
- Progress monitoring and reassessment
- Family coaching on language strategies
- Collaboration with school and facility staff

**Multilingual Development Support:**
- Balance between native language maintenance and host language acquisition
- Exposure to both languages in home and school settings
- Professional guidance on multilingual development
- Prevention of language mixing confusion
- Assessment of proficiency in both languages
- Family education on supporting bilingual development

**Care Adjustments:**
- School programming adjusted for language level
- Extra language support in classroom if needed
- Home language practice strategies provided
- Peer interaction facilitation for language development
- Cultural materials and activities supporting both languages

**Expected Outcomes by Month 48 (Age 4):**
- Functional proficiency in host language
- Age-appropriate vocabulary development
- Ability to communicate in classroom setting
- Native language connection maintained
- Any speech concerns identified and in treatment
- Clear trajectory toward language proficiency
- Multilingual balance achieved

**Tuteur Actions:**
- Arrange initial and ongoing language assessment
- Coordinate speech/language therapy if indicated
- Monitor language progress regularly
- Support home language practice
- Communicate with school about language needs
- Document language development in case file
- Facilitate peer interactions for language practice

## 7. Language Development

### Assessment Tools & Framework

**Professional Language Assessment Components:**
- Native language proficiency level (receptive and expressive)
- Host language (French) proficiency level
- Speech sound accuracy and intelligibility
- Vocabulary size and word types (nouns, verbs, adjectives)
- Grammar and sentence structure complexity
- Pragmatic language skills (conversation, social use)
- Narrative and storytelling abilities

**Standardized Assessment Tools:**
- Bilingual Input Output Scales (BIOS) - for multilingual children
- Peabody Picture Vocabulary Test (PPVT) - vocabulary assessment
- Clinical Evaluation of Language Fundamentals (CELF) - comprehensive language
- Preschool Language Scale (PLS) - developmental language assessment
- Speech-language pathology evaluations - sound production and fluency

**Progress Monitoring:**
- Quarterly language assessment by speech professional
- Teacher observations of classroom language use
- Family observations of home language development
- Vocabulary tracking and documentation
- Sentence complexity monitoring
- Social communication skill assessment
- Reading/pre-literacy skill development (ages 4-6)

### Training Programs & Resources

**Language Instruction Approaches:**

*Immersion-Based:*
- Full daily kindergarten program in host language
- Limited native language use at school
- Natural acquisition through exposure and interaction
- Effective for children ages 2-6 (critical period advantage)
- Staff fluent and trained in early language instruction

*Bilingual Programs:*
- Structured exposure to both languages during day
- Native language support (1-2 hours per week)
- Bilingual teachers or language specialists
- Supports language maintenance while building host language
- Gradual shift in language balance over time

*Language Tutoring/Therapy:*
- Private speech-language therapy (1-2x weekly if indicated)
- Focused on specific language development goals
- Small group or individual sessions
- Parent coaching and home activities
- Progress-based frequency adjustments

**Home Language Activities:**
- Storybook reading in native language (20-30 min daily)
- Conversational practice with family
- Cultural celebrations and family traditions
- Video calls with family speaking native language
- Music and songs in native language
- Traditional games and activities from culture

**School Language Support:**
- Classroom modifications for language learners
- Peer buddies or language partners
- Visual supports and simplified language
- Repetition and reinforcement opportunities
- Teacher training on second language acquisition
- Curriculum adjustments for language development

**Professional Services:**
- Speech-language pathologist (€80-150 per session)
- Bilingual language specialist (€60-100 per session)
- Kindergarten special language program (€100-150/month if needed)
- Teacher consultation and coaching (included in school)

**Expected Language Proficiency by Age 6:**
- Functional communication in host language
- Vocabulary of 1,500+ words in each language
- Complex sentence structures and grammar
- Ability to participate in classroom instruction
- Pre-literacy skills developing
- Cultural and linguistic identity maintained
- Readiness for primary school instruction

## 8. Family Contact Development

### Structure & Protocol

**Initial Family Assessment:**
- Determine if family location is known and safely accessible
- Assess safety and welfare concerns related to contact
- Evaluate quality of prior relationship and attachment
- Consider trauma or separation circumstances
- Document family wishes regarding contact
- Assess child's emotional capacity for contact

**Supervised Contact Programs:**
- Family contact center services (Red Cross, government agencies)
- Professional staff trained in facilitating contact
- Neutral setting for visits or video contact
- Safety protocols and supervision procedures
- Frequency: typically 1-2x monthly depending on circumstances
- Duration: 1-2 hours per contact session
- Documentation of all contact sessions

**Contact Frequency Guidelines:**
- Newly located family: start with monthly contact
- Established contact: bi-weekly to monthly
- Strong positive relationships: potentially more frequent
- Concerning dynamics: limited supervised contact only
- Video/phone contact: between in-person visits if possible
- Assessment-based adjustments as needed

**Communication Facilitation:**

## 12. Phase Transition Criteria

**Transition to Phase 3 (6-11 Years) occurs when:**
- Child reaches 72 months (6 years) chronological age or equivalent developmental stage
- Completion of kindergarten or pre-primary program
- Readiness for primary school established
- Language proficiency in host language functional
- Social integration and peer relationships established
- All outstanding health and developmental concerns addressed
- Educational and social development plan established for primary school
- Family status determined (reunification, permanence, or ongoing contact plan)
- Comprehensive 6-year assessment completed

- Health examination and vaccination records
- Developmental and behavioral assessment
- Educational readiness evaluation
- School placement and enrollment confirmation
