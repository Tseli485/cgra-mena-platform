# Obligations Summary Module
Quick Reference for Legal, Financial & Administrative Duties

---

## Legal Obligations at a Glance

### Registration Requirements
- Initial MENA registration with local judicial authority (within 30 days of appointment)
- Child identification documentation (birth certificate, ID card)
- Guardianship decision registration in civil registry
- Annual renewal/confirmation of guardianship status
- Notification of any change in living situation or contact information

### Mandatory Reporting
- Report any signs of abuse, neglect, or exploitation (to social services)
- Notify authorities of changes in family circumstances
- Disclose conflicts of interest or guardianship limitations
- Report financial irregularities or mismanagement suspicions
- Communicate health emergencies within 24 hours

### Documentation Duties
- Maintain complete file on each child (medical, educational, legal records)
- Keep copies of all guardianship-related documents
- Document all major decisions and their justifications
- Record communications with child, family members, authorities
- Preserve evidence of child's integration progress

### Reporting Obligations
- Annual legal report to judicial authority (progress, challenges, needs)
- Quarterly financial statements (if managing funds)
- School performance reports to relevant authorities
- Medical updates for children with health complications
- Incident reports within 48 hours (accidents, behavioral issues)

### Cooperation with Authorities
- Attend mandatory training and oversight meetings
- Respond to inspection visits from social services
- Provide information requested by judicial authorities
- Participate in case reviews and family meetings
- Collaborate with educational and health professionals

## Financial Obligations Quick View

| Obligation | Monthly Budget | Allocation | Management | Documentation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Accommodation** | 30-40% | Housing, utilities, maintenance | Rent/mortgage payment records | Invoices, contracts, receipts |
| **Nutrition** | 20-25% | Food, groceries, meal prep | Purchase at approved vendors | Receipts, meal plans, menus |
| **Education** | 15-20% | School fees, materials, tutoring | Direct school payments or approved providers | Enrollment, fee receipts, report cards |
| **Healthcare** | 10-15% | Medical, dental, medications | Authorized medical facilities | Prescriptions, clinic records, insurance |
| **Clothing & Hygiene** | 5-10% | Seasonal clothing, toiletries | Age-appropriate purchases | Receipts, inventory records |
| **Transportation** | 5-8% | School/medical visits, essential travel | Public transport or authorized vehicles | Receipts, travel logs |
| **Personal Development** | 5-10% | Sports, arts, recreation, counseling | Approved activities list | Enrollment confirmations, fee receipts |
| **Emergency Reserve** | 5% | Unexpected needs, contingencies | Separated account or escrow | Bank statements, justification documents |

### Account Management Requirements
- Establish dedicated account(s) for child's funds (separate from personal finances)
- All withdrawals documented and justified
- Monthly reconciliation of income vs. expenses
- Annual audit trail available for inspection
- Interest earned on reserves credited to child's account

### Documentation Needed
- Bank statements (monthly)
- Receipt folder (organized by category and month)
- Budget tracking spreadsheet
- Invoices and payment proof for major expenses
- Authorization letters for fund access (if applicable)
- Expense justification forms for discretionary spending

## Document Requirements Checklist

### Essential Documents by Phase

**INITIAL INTAKE (First 30 Days)**
- [ ] Guardianship decision/court order
- [ ] Child's birth certificate
- [ ] Child's identity document (if issued)
- [ ] Medical examination results
- [ ] Family contact information and status
- [ ] Educational history/previous school records
- [ ] Social services assessment report

**ONGOING DOCUMENTATION**
- [ ] Medical records folder (vaccinations, check-ups, treatment)
- [ ] Educational records (enrollment, grades, attendance)
- [ ] Financial ledger (updated monthly)
- [ ] Incident log (accidents, behavioral issues, achievements)
- [ ] Family communication log (visits, calls, letters)
- [ ] Training certificates (guardianship, first aid, safeguarding)

**LEGAL COMPLIANCE**
- [ ] Guardianship renewal documentation
- [ ] Authority inspection reports and responses
- [ ] Court correspondence and legal updates
- [ ] Consent forms (medical, educational, travel)
- [ ] Insurance policies and proof of coverage

### Where to Obtain
| Document | Source | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Birth Certificate | Local civil registry | Within 7 days of request |
| ID Card | National ID authority | 30-90 days processing |
| Medical Records | Healthcare provider | Upon request |
| School Documents | Educational institution | Within 5 working days |
| Court Orders | Judicial authority | Available after decision |
| Social Services Reports | Local social services | On demand |

### Storage Recommendations
- **Physical Files**: Locked cabinet with alphabetical organization
- **Digital Backup**: Encrypted folder on secure drive or cloud (password-protected)
- **Accessibility**: Child records available within 24 hours upon request
- **Retention**: Keep all documents for minimum 5 years after guardianship ends
- **Privacy**: Restricted access—only authorized personnel (child, authorities, professionals)

## Timeline Overview: Key Dates & Deadlines

### FIRST MONTH (Days 1-30)
| Date | Task | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Day 1** | Register guardianship decision | Critical |
| **Day 5** | Schedule initial medical examination | Critical |
| **Day 10** | Enroll in school (if applicable) | Critical |
| **Day 15** | Open dedicated bank account | High |
| **Day 20** | Establish filing system | High |
| **Day 25** | Schedule first family meeting (if applicable) | Medium |
| **Day 30** | Submit initial report to authorities | Critical |

### FIRST YEAR MILESTONES
| Month | Milestone | Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Month 2** | First monthly financial report | Review budget, adjust allocations |
| **Month 3** | First school progress review | Meet with teachers, assess needs |
| **Month 6** | Mid-year medical check-up | Update health records, vaccinations |
| **Month 9** | Educational assessment | Plan for next academic year |
| **Month 12** | Annual legal report due | Compile year's documentation, submit to authorities |

### AGE MILESTONE DEADLINES

**At Age 6 (School Entry)**
- Confirm compulsory education enrollment
- Update guardianship status with school district
- Schedule educational assessment if needed
- Review and update emergency contacts

**At Age 12 (Adolescence)**
- Transition to secondary education planning
- Initiate vocational interest assessment
- Update medical records and vaccinations
- Begin financial literacy education

**At Age 16 (Work-Eligible)**
- Obtain work permit if applicable (jurisdiction-dependent)
- Update guardianship documentation
- Begin transition to independence planning
- Initiate driver's license/identity renewal process

**At Age 18 (Legal Adulthood Approach)**
- Prepare emancipation/independence plan
- Conduct comprehensive life skills assessment
- Finalize educational/vocational pathway
- Establish post-guardianship support network
