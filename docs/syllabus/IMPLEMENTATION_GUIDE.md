# IMPLEMENTATION GUIDE: 4-Week Training Curriculum Using the CGRA Syllabus System

## Overview

This guide provides a complete 4-week training program for new staff, using the CGRA Syllabus and Dossiers as the training materials. The curriculum covers all essential knowledge and skills needed for effective practice.

---

## Quick Reference: 4-Week Overview

```
WEEK 1: FOUNDATIONS
├─ Day 1-2: Legal framework & obligations (dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/)
├─ Day 3: Emergency procedures & contacts (dossiers/05_Ressources/)
└─ Day 4-5: Role responsibilities & ethics

WEEK 2: PROCEDURES & SYSTEMS
├─ Day 1-2: Asylum procedures (dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/)
├─ Day 3: Family contact procedures
├─ Day 4: School enrollment procedures
└─ Day 5: Housing & services access procedures

WEEK 3: REAL-WORLD APPLICATION
├─ Day 1-3: Case study analysis (dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/)
├─ Day 4: Supervision meeting & feedback
└─ Day 5: Shadow experienced staff member

WEEK 4: INTEGRATION & ASSESSMENT
├─ Day 1-3: Continued case study work & supervision
├─ Day 4: Practical scenarios and problem-solving
└─ Day 5: Assessment & readiness verification
```

## WEEK 1: FOUNDATIONS

### Learning Objectives

By the end of Week 1, trainees will be able to:
1. Explain the legal framework governing child protection
2. Identify their core obligations as a guardian/tutor
3. Describe what constitutes abuse and when to report
4. Access emergency contacts and resources
5. Understand the ethical foundation of the work

### Daily Schedule

---

#### **DAY 1: Legal Framework & Child Rights**

**Morning (2 hours)**:
- Icebreaker & orientation (30 min)
- Present: SYLLABUS_COURTE.pdf overview (30 min)
  - Main topics and their importance
  - How to navigate the system
  - Where to find help
- Discuss: Why legal framework matters (30 min)

**Activity**:
- Read: dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/README.md (20 min)
- Read: Droits_MENA.md (40 min)
- Discussion: "What rights does the child have? What are your obligations?" (20 min)

**Afternoon (2 hours)**:
- Read: dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/Obligations_Legales_Detailed.md (60 min)
- Review: Key obligations section (30 min)
- Group discussion: "What surprised you? What questions do you have?" (30 min)

**Homework**:
- List the 5 most important legal obligations you learned about
- Find dossiers/05_Ressources/Contacts_Urgence.md on your computer
- Memorize at least the emergency number (17) and child protection contact

---

#### **DAY 2: Duty of Care & Mandatory Reporting**

**Morning (2 hours)**:
- Review homework from Day 1 (15 min)
- Teach: Duty of Care concept (45 min)
  - What it means legally
  - What it means practically
  - Examples and non-examples
- Activity: Case examples
  - Present 3 scenarios about child safety
  - Ask: "Is the tutor meeting duty of care? Why/why not?"
  - Discuss implications (60 min)

**Afternoon (2.5 hours)**:
- Teach: Mandatory Reporting (45 min)
  - When you must report
  - What constitutes abuse
  - How to report
  - Your legal protection
- Activity: Role play
  - Scenario 1: Physical abuse indicator
  - Scenario 2: Neglect indicator
  - Scenario 3: Emotional abuse indicator
  - Practice: "What would you do?" (75 min)

**Assignment**:
- Complete Case_Analysis_Worksheet.md for one practice scenario
- Write out: "I would report abuse if... [your examples]"
- Submit to supervisor for feedback

---

#### **DAY 3: Emergency Procedures & Resources**

**Morning (1.5 hours)**:
- Teach: Emergency response procedures (45 min)
  - What constitutes emergency
  - Who to call and when
  - What information to provide
  - Your role after reporting
- Practical training: Emergency contacts
  - Map out dossiers/05_Ressources/Contacts_Urgence.md
  - Practice dialing procedures
  - Review both written and digital access (45 min)

**Activity**:
- Scavenger hunt: Find 5 specific resources in dossiers/05_Ressources/ (45 min)
- Quiz: Can you find contact for [X service] in 2 minutes? (20 min)

**Assignment**:
- Laminate your own copy of Contacts_Urgence.md
- Memorize emergency numbers (create flashcards)
- Identify which resources are most relevant to your specific role

---

#### **DAY 4-5: Role Responsibilities & Professional Ethics**

**Day 4 Morning (2 hours)**:
- Teach: Professional responsibilities (45 min)
  - Confidentiality
  - Documentation
  - Professional boundaries
  - Continuing education
- Review: dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/Checklist_Tuteur_Detailed.md (45 min)
  - Daily responsibilities
  - Weekly responsibilities
  - Monthly and annual requirements

**Day 4 Afternoon (2 hours)**:
- Teach: Professional ethics (45 min)
  - Honesty about qualifications
  - Competence boundaries
  - Conflict resolution
  - Ethical decision-making
- Case discussion: Ethics scenarios (75 min)
  - Scenario: Child asks you not to tell anyone something
  - Scenario: Parent asks you to do something outside your role
  - Scenario: You don't understand something but don't want to ask

**Day 5 Morning (2 hours)**:
- Review: Week 1 key concepts (45 min)
- Open Q&A: What do you need to understand better? (45 min)
- Practical simulation: Emergency call role-play
  - You receive call that child is missing
  - Practice your response
  - Debrief: What would you do? (30 min)

**Day 5 Afternoon (1 hour)**:
- Assessment: Week 1 quiz (20 questions)
- Debrief: Results and next steps
- Assign Week 2 materials

**Assessment Rubric: Week 1**:
- [ ] Can explain legal framework (questions 1-3)
- [ ] Can identify abuse indicators (questions 4-6)
- [ ] Can state mandatory reporting procedure (questions 7-9)
- [ ] Knows emergency contacts (questions 10-12)
- [ ] Understands professional obligations (questions 13-20)

**Pass Requirement**: 80% (16/20 questions correct)

## WEEK 2: PROCEDURES & SYSTEMS

### Learning Objectives

By the end of Week 2, trainees will be able to:
1. Explain the complete asylum procedure from start to finish
2. Describe family contact and family reunification procedures
3. Explain school enrollment procedures
4. Describe housing and service access procedures
5. Identify which procedure applies to different situations

### Daily Schedule

---

#### **DAY 1-2: Asylum Procedure Deep Dive**

**Day 1 (4 hours)**:

**Morning**:
- Introduction to asylum system (45 min)
  - Why asylum exists
  - Key actors (CGRA, prefecture, etc.)
  - Timeline overview
  - Common challenges
- Read: dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/Procedure_Asile_Complete.md (90 min)
- Map activity: Create visual timeline of asylum procedure on whiteboard (45 min)

**Afternoon**:
- Forms overview: dossiers/02_Formulaires_CGRA/ (60 min)
  - What each form is for
  - When it's used
  - What information it collects
- Practice activity: Fill out sample asylum form (60 min)

**Day 2 (4 hours)**:

**Morning**:
- Teach: Interview preparation & documentation (60 min)
  - Preparing child for asylum interview
  - What interviewer is looking for
  - How tutor can help child prepare
- Role play: Practice asylum interview (75 min)
  - Trainer plays CGRA interviewer
  - Trainee plays tutor supporting child
  - Focus on: communication, timing, documentation

**Afternoon**:
- Case example: Review real asylum case from dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/
  - Identify decision points
  - Note timelines
  - Recognize procedures used (60 min)
- Q&A: Any asylum-related questions? (60 min)

**Assignment**:
- Complete Case_Analysis_Worksheet for asylum case
- Create personal asylum checklist (steps you would take)
- Identify questions you still have

---

#### **DAY 3: Family Contact & Reunification**

**Morning (2 hours)**:
- Teach: Family contact principles (45 min)
  - When family contact is beneficial
  - When family contact is risky
  - Safety assessment
  - Documentation requirements
- Read: dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/Procedure_Famille.md (45 min)

**Afternoon (2 hours)**:
- Case study: dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/CASE_002_family_conflict.md (45 min)
- Scenario: Family requests contact
  - Assess: Safe or unsafe contact?
  - Plan: How would you facilitate?
  - Prepare: What information is needed?
  - Document: What would you write down? (75 min)

**Assignment**:
- Complete Case_Analysis_Worksheet for family scenario
- Draft letter to family using dossiers/06_Templates_Lettres/Lettre_Famille.md

---

#### **DAY 4: School Enrollment & Education Access**

**Morning (2 hours)**:
- Teach: Education rights and procedures (45 min)
  - Legal right to education
  - Language assessment
  - Grade placement
  - Special needs identification
- Read: School enrollment procedure (if exists) or Procedure_Asile_Complete.md education section (45 min)

**Afternoon (2 hours)**:
- Practical activity: Find schools in your area (45 min)
  - Online research: Which schools accept new arrivals?
  - Contact: What information do you need?
  - Requirements: What documentation is needed?
- Draft letter to school using dossiers/06_Templates_Lettres/Lettre_Ecole.md (75 min)

**Assignment**:
- Identify 3 schools in your area that might accept referrals
- Create enrollment checklist for your area
- Find translation services contact (many children need language support)

---

#### **DAY 5: Housing & Services Access**

**Morning (2 hours)**:
- Teach: Housing and basic services (45 min)
  - Access to shelter/housing
  - Healthcare access
  - Social services coordination
  - Financial support available
- Read: dossiers/05_Ressources/Services.md and Organisations.md (45 min)

**Afternoon (2 hours)**:
- Practical activity: Resource mapping
  - In your area: Find housing resources
  - In your area: Find healthcare providers
  - In your area: Find social services agencies
  - Create reference document for future use (90 min)

**Week 2 Assessment: Procedure Scenarios**:

Present 5 scenarios; trainee identifies:
1. Which procedure applies
2. First 3 steps
3. Who needs to be contacted
4. Timeline/deadline

Scenarios:
1. Child arrives unaccompanied, needs asylum claim
2. Child's family contacts from country of origin
3. 6-year-old needs school enrollment
4. 17-year-old has no stable housing
5. Child needs medical treatment

**Pass Requirement**: Can correctly identify procedure and first 3 steps for all 5 scenarios

---

## WEEK 3: REAL-WORLD APPLICATION

### Learning Objectives

By the end of Week 3, trainees will be able to:
1. Analyze complex case scenarios
2. Identify issues and develop response plans
3. Apply learning from Week 1-2 to realistic situations
4. Demonstrate decision-making and documentation skills
5. Receive and incorporate feedback

### Daily Schedule

---

#### **DAY 1-3: Case Study Analysis & Supervision**

**Format**: Each case takes 1 full day

**Structure for Each Case (3 hours per day)**:

1. **Case Introduction** (15 min)
   - Background of the child/situation
   - Key challenges
   - Questions to answer

2. **Individual Analysis** (60 min)
   - Trainee completes Case_Analysis_Worksheet.md
   - Identifies issues
   - Develops response plan
   - Documents decision-making

3. **Group Discussion** (45 min)
   - Trainee presents their analysis (10 min)
   - Group questions and feedback (20 min)
   - Alternative approaches discussed (10 min)
   - Supervisor provides expert perspective (5 min)

4. **Debrief & Learning** (30 min)
   - What was challenging about this case?
   - What would you do differently?
   - How does this connect to Week 1-2 learning?

**Cases to Analyze** (Pick 3 from dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/):
- CASE_001_unaccompanied.md (child arrival & initial assessment)
- CASE_003_trauma.md (trauma response)
- CASE_004_runaway.md (missing child emergency)
- CASE_005_work_situation.md (labor/exploitation concern)
- CASE_007_health.md (health emergency)

---

#### **DAY 4: Supervision Meeting & Feedback**

**Morning (2 hours)**:
- Individual supervision meeting with supervisor (45 min)
  - Review Week 1-3 progress
  - Feedback on case analyses
  - Strengths and areas for development
  - Address any concerns or questions

- Group reflection (75 min)
  - What was hardest this week?
  - What feels clearer now?
  - What do you still need to understand?
  - How are you feeling about the role?

**Afternoon (1.5 hours)**:
- Role play: Difficult conversation scenarios
  - Scenario 1: Telling parent you must report concern
  - Scenario 2: Explaining procedure to child
  - Scenario 3: Resisting request outside your role
  - Feedback and coaching

---

#### **DAY 5: Shadow Experienced Staff**

**Morning & Afternoon (4 hours)**:
- Observe experienced tutor/staff member with child
- Take notes on:
  - How they create safe environment
  - How they communicate with child
  - How they handle transitions
  - How they manage challenging behavior
  - How they document
  - Questions they ask and don't ask

**Debrief** (1 hour):
- Reflect with shadowed staff member
- Ask: How did you handle that situation?
- Connect to: What would you do?

---

## WEEK 4: INTEGRATION & ASSESSMENT

### Learning Objectives

By the end of Week 4, trainees will be able to:
1. Handle common situations independently
2. Solve novel problems using training framework
3. Demonstrate all competencies from Weeks 1-3
4. Plan ongoing professional development
5. Become active team member

### Daily Schedule

---

#### **DAY 1-3: Advanced Case Work & Practical Scenarios**

**Day 1: Complex Multi-Issue Case**:

Present case with multiple, intersecting issues:
- Example: 14-year-old who (1) hasn't attended school, (2) shows trauma signs, (3) has expressed unsafe thoughts, (4) wants family contact with mother in unsafe country

Trainee:
- Identifies all issues
- Prioritizes them
- Develops comprehensive response plan
- Considers timeline and sequencing
- Coordinates multiple procedures
- Completes full Case_Analysis_Worksheet

Debrief with supervisor

---

**Day 2: Problem-Solving Scenarios**:

Present novel situations (not in training materials):
- Scenario 1: You notice child has bruise; child won't explain; parent has reasonable explanation
  - Do you report? Why or why not?
  - What do you do?
  - How do you document?

- Scenario 2: Child's school says child is not attending; parent says child is home sick
  - What's the issue?
  - Who do you contact?
  - What procedure applies?
  - What are timelines?

- Scenario 3: You're not sure if something requires reporting
  - What do you do?
  - Who do you consult?
  - How do you decide?
  - What factors matter?

Trainee reasons through each scenario, supervisor provides feedback

---

**Day 3: Integration with Team**:

- Join team meeting or case consultation
- Participate in discussion
- Observe how team applies learning
- Ask questions
- Begin informal consultation about assignment

---

#### **DAY 4: Practical Competency Assessment**

**Written Assessment** (2 hours):
- Answer 10 scenario-based questions
- Each scenario tests application of Week 1-4 learning
- Open-book (can consult materials)

**Scoring Rubric**:
- Does trainee identify relevant legal/procedural framework?
- Does trainee identify correct procedure?
- Does trainee explain reasoning?
- Does trainee anticipate complications?
- Does trainee plan appropriate documentation?

Pass Requirement: 80% (8/10 scenarios answered adequately)

---

**Observation Assessment** (1 hour):
- Supervisor observes trainee in direct interaction
- Look for:
  - Appropriate child engagement
  - Safety awareness
  - Professional demeanor
  - Boundary maintenance
  - Communication skills

Pass Requirement: Supervisor confirmation that trainee demonstrates competence

---

**Documentation Review** (30 min):
- Review all Case_Analysis_Worksheets completed during training
- Assess:
  - Completeness
  - Clarity of thinking
  - Appropriate identification of issues
  - Reasonable decision-making

Pass Requirement: Overall quality demonstrates understanding

---

#### **DAY 5: Completion & Readiness Verification**

**Morning (1.5 hours)**:

**One-on-One with Supervisor**:
- Review all assessment results
- Discuss readiness for independent practice
- Identify any areas needing further support
- Create Ongoing Development Plan

**Readiness Checklist**:
- [ ] Understands legal obligations and reporting requirements
- [ ] Can access emergency contacts and resources
- [ ] Can navigate major procedures
- [ ] Can analyze case scenarios and identify issues
- [ ] Can document appropriately
- [ ] Can ask for help when uncertain
- [ ] Demonstrated professional communication
- [ ] Ready for assignment with ongoing supervision

---

**Afternoon (1.5 hours)**:

**Completion Celebration & Future Planning**:
- Certificate of Completion (if organizational policy)
- Assign initial caseload or role (if ready)
- Set first supervision appointment
- Distribute materials for independent use:
  - Individual binder with essential materials
  - Digital access to full system
  - Supervisor's contact information
  - Schedule for ongoing training/supervision

**Ongoing Development Plan**:
- Identify specific areas for continued learning
- Schedule quarterly supervision focus topics
- Plan continued education/training
- Identify mentoring relationships
- Set realistic goals for first 3 months

## Assessment Rubrics

### Week 1 Assessment Rubric: Legal Framework & Obligations

| Competency | Proficient | Developing | Needs Improvement |
|------------|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Legal Framework Knowledge** | Can explain legal framework and core obligations accurately | Can explain with minor gaps | Cannot accurately explain framework |
| **Abuse Identification** | Can identify all 4 types of abuse with specific indicators | Can identify 3 types with some indicators | Cannot reliably identify abuse signs |
| **Reporting Procedure** | Knows when, how, and to whom to report; understands legal protection | Knows basic reporting procedure; some uncertainty | Unclear on reporting process |
| **Emergency Response** | Knows emergency contacts; can explain priority procedures | Knows contacts; some uncertainty on procedures | Does not reliably access emergency contacts |
| **Professional Ethics** | Understands confidentiality, boundaries, and ethical decision-making | Generally understands with some questions | Demonstrates gaps in ethical understanding |

---

### Week 2 Assessment Rubric: Procedures

| Competency | Proficient | Developing | Needs Improvement |
|------------|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Asylum Procedure** | Can explain all steps, timelines, and key documents | Can explain main steps; gaps on details | Limited understanding of asylum process |
| **Family Procedure** | Can assess safety and explain contact protocol | Understands basics; uncertain on assessment | Gaps in family procedure knowledge |
| **School Enrollment** | Can identify requirements and document needs | Knows process; some uncertainty on details | Limited knowledge of enrollment requirements |
| **Resource Access** | Can identify and access services in own area | Knows some resources; needs more information | Limited resource knowledge |
| **Procedure Selection** | Can correctly match situation to procedure 90%+ of the time | Can identify procedure 70-80% of time | Cannot reliably identify correct procedure |

---

### Week 3 Assessment Rubric: Case Analysis & Application

| Competency | Proficient | Developing | Needs Improvement |
|------------|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Issue Identification** | Identifies all major issues and some nuances | Identifies primary issues; may miss secondary | Misses important issues |
| **Legal/Procedural Application** | Correctly identifies applicable laws/procedures | Identifies procedure; some gaps in reasoning | Cannot reliably identify relevant framework |
| **Decision-Making** | Makes logical, well-reasoned decisions that prioritize child safety | Makes reasonable decisions; some gaps in reasoning | Decisions lack clear reasoning |
| **Documentation** | Completes worksheet thoroughly with clear, specific language | Completes worksheet; some gaps in detail | Incomplete or unclear documentation |
| **Problem-Solving** | Develops creative solutions; considers alternatives and consequences | Develops workable solution; limited consideration of alternatives | Struggles to develop adequate response plan |

---

### Week 4 Assessment Rubric: Readiness for Practice

| Competency | Proficient | Developing | Needs Improvement |
|------------|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Written Assessment** | 80-100% of scenarios answered adequately | 70-79% of scenarios answered adequately | Below 70%; needs remediation |
| **Observation Assessment** | Demonstrates competence in observed situation | Generally appropriate; needs some coaching | Significant concerns about readiness |
| **Documentation Quality** | Clear, complete, objective documentation | Generally adequate; some detail gaps | Gaps in quality or completeness |
| **Professional Demeanor** | Demonstrates professional communication and boundary-maintenance | Generally professional; occasional lapses | Concerns about professionalism |
| **Overall Readiness** | Ready for assignment with ongoing supervision | Ready with close supervision and mentoring | Not yet ready; needs remediation |

## Ongoing Supervision & Professional Development

### After the 4-Week Training

**Month 1-3: Close Supervision**:
- Weekly 1:1 supervision meetings (1 hour)
- Discuss specific cases and decisions
- Review documentation
- Address any concerns
- Begin assigning increasing independence

**Month 4-6: Regular Supervision**:
- Bi-weekly 1:1 supervision meetings
- Less case-by-case, more pattern/development focus
- Begin group supervision
- Support continued learning

**Month 6+: Ongoing Support**:
- Monthly 1:1 supervision
- Quarterly group supervision
- Annual performance review
- Continued training and professional development

**First Year Training Topics**:
- Month 2: Trauma-informed care
- Month 3: Working with adolescents
- Month 4: Family dynamics and engagement
- Month 5: Documentation and confidentiality
- Month 6: Professional self-care
- Month 9: Legal updates and case law
- Month 12: Annual reflection and planning

## Customizing for Your Context

This 4-week program is a template. Customize based on:

1. **Your Organization's Needs**
   - Add local procedures
   - Emphasize roles specific to your organization
   - Include local case examples

2. **Your Audience**
   - More experienced trainees: Move faster, add complexity
   - Less experienced trainees: Slow down, add repetition
   - Different roles: Adjust procedures covered

3. **Your Resources**
   - Full-time vs. part-time training
   - Individual vs. group training
   - Remote vs. in-person
   - Budget for materials

4. **Your Context**
   - Local legal framework
   - Local procedures and agencies
   - Local resource landscape
   - Cultural and language considerations
