# Belgian Financial Institutions for Minors

## Overview
This document provides detailed information about Belgian banks that accept minors as account holders, account types available, documentation requirements, and procedures for opening accounts for children under guardianship.

---

## 1. ING Belgium

### Institution Details
- **Full Name**: ING Belgium NV
- **Headquarters**: Brussels
- **Presence**: 200+ branches nationwide
- **Website**: www.ing.be
- **Customer Service**: +32 (0)2 464 64 64
- **Minors Program**: ING Kids Account

### Account Types for Minors

#### ING Kids Account (Age 0-18)
- **Minimum Age**: No minimum (parents can open for newborns)
- **Joint Ownership**: Parent/Guardian as primary, child as co-owner
- **Monthly Fee**: €0
- **Features**:
  - Savings account with 2-3% annual interest
  - Mobile app access (limited to guardian until age 12)
  - Automatic transfers for allowance deposits
  - No overdraft capability
  - ATM card available from age 10
  - Debit card available from age 12

#### Youth Account (Age 12-18)
- **Minimum Age**: 12 years
- **Monthly Fee**: €0
- **Features**:
  - Full debit card with €500/month spending limit
  - Online banking access (with parental controls)
  - Direct deposit setup
  - Savings sub-account
  - Digital wallet (Apple Pay, Google Pay)
  - No overdraft

#### Stepping Stone Account (Age 18-25)
- Transitional account for young adults
- Lower fees and better rates than standard adult accounts
- Available immediately upon 18th birthday

### Documents Required to Open Account

**For Guardian:**
1. Valid ID (Belgian ID card, passport, or residence permit)
2. Proof of address (utility bill from last 3 months)
3. Proof of income (payslip, self-employment documentation)
4. Proof of guardianship (if not biological parent):
   - Court guardianship order
   - Adoption certificate
   - Legal separation/divorce decree naming guardian
5. Guardianship documentation from local municipality (Gemeente/Commune)

**For Child:**
1. Birth certificate (original or certified copy)
2. ID number (Belgian registration number)
3. Proof of residence in Belgium

### Account Opening Process
1. **Appointment Booking**: Contact local branch or book online
2. **Documentation Review**: Typically 15-30 minutes in-branch
3. **Account Activation**: Same day or within 24 hours
4. **Card Ordering**: Physical card arrives in 5-7 business days
5. **Welcome Package**: Includes PIN, account details, educational materials

### Special Features for Guardians
- Parental controls accessible via mobile app
- Real-time spending notifications
- Automatic blocking of certain merchant categories
- Monthly statements for accounting purposes
- Annual tax statement for dependent claims

### Contact Information
- **Local Branches**: Use branch locator on www.ing.be
- **Minor Accounts Team**: +32 (0)2 464 70 70
- **Email Support**: kids@ing.be (response time: 24 hours)

## 7. Special Circumstances

### Guardianship from CPAS/Government
When child is in government guardianship (through CPAS):
- Special documentation required from CPAS certifying guardianship
- Account may require court-authorized access restrictions
- Monthly reporting requirements to CPAS
- All banks accept CPAS guardianship documentation
- **Recommended**: KBC or Belfius (best experience with institutional guardianship)

### Multiple Guardians
If co-guardianship exists (both parents or multiple guardians):
- Both guardians must sign opening documents
- Both receive access to online banking
- Spending limits apply regardless of which guardian approves
- Recommended: Automatic approval notification to both guardians

### Child with Special Needs
Enhanced documentation may include:
- Medical assessment
- Disability documentation
- Capacity evaluation (if relevant)
- Special accommodation requests
- Power of attorney documentation
- **Recommended**: BNP Paribas Fortis (best accommodations for special needs)

## 9. Best Practices for Guardian Account Management

### Monthly Responsibilities
1. Review all transactions for accuracy
2. Verify allowance deposits received
3. Check spending against approved limits
4. Document expenses for tax purposes
5. Communicate account status with child (age-appropriate)

### Annual Tasks
1. Request and file tax documentation
2. Review account adequacy for child's needs
3. Assess spending limits and adjust as needed
4. Verify guardian controls still appropriate
5. Consider account upgrade as child ages

### Transition to Independence (Age 18)
- Transfer account to adult product (usually automatic)
- Gradual removal of guardian controls
- Financial education on independent banking
- Discussion of credit history and responsible borrowing
- Transition of account access from guardian to young adult
