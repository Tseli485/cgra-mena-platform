# PRINTING GUIDE: How to Print and Organize the CGRA Syllabus System

## Overview

This guide helps you decide what to print, how to organize it, and when to use digital vs. print versions.

---

## Strategic Printing Decisions

### Principle 1: Print for Accessibility, Not Comprehensiveness

**Print:**
- Documents you reference frequently (daily/weekly)
- Emergency contacts and crisis procedures
- Quick checklists and decision trees
- Materials needed in situations with no computer access

**Don't Print:**
- Lengthy training materials (read digitally)
- Infrequently-used templates (print as needed)
- Documents with many cross-references (digital is better)
- Large case studies (better for reading digitally)

---

### Principle 2: Digital Advantages

**Keep Digital**:
- Searchable text (find information quickly)
- Hyperlinks and cross-references (quick navigation)
- Updated versions (no old printouts to confuse)
- Secure storage with backup
- Multimedia (videos, links to resources)

**Examples**:
- SYLLABUS_COMPLETE.pdf (80 pages - use digital)
- dossiers/05_Ressources/Organisations.md (frequently updated - keep digital)
- dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/ (long case studies - read on screen)

---

### Principle 3: Print for Durability & Accessibility

**Print & Laminate**:
- Emergency contacts (dossiers/05_Ressources/Contacts_Urgence.md)
- Critical procedures (keep near your desk)
- Laminated for durability and water-resistance

**Print & Bind**:
- Your organization's policies and procedures
- Procedure guides you use weekly
- Reference manual for each staff member

**Print & Share**:
- Individual checklists for families
- Resource guides for client distribution
- Translated materials for clients

## Recommended Printing Plan

### PRIORITY TIER 1: ESSENTIAL (Print Immediately)

**Who Should Have This**: All staff

**What to Print**:
1. **SYLLABUS_COURTE.pdf** (20 pages)
   - Print: 1 copy per staff member
   - Binding: Spiral or stapled
   - Cost: Low (20 pages × number of staff)
   - Access: Keep at desk

2. **dossiers/05_Ressources/Contacts_Urgence.md** (2-4 pages)
   - Print: 2-3 copies per staff member (desk + wallet/bag)
   - Laminate: YES - for durability
   - Cost: Very low
   - Access: Keep accessible at all times

3. **dossiers/01_Documents_Tuteur/Checklist_Tuteur.md** (1-2 pages)
   - Print: 5 copies per staff member (daily use)
   - Binding: None (single sheets)
   - Cost: Minimal
   - Access: Use with each child daily

**Printing Instructions**:
- Single-sided or double-sided: Double-sided saves paper and binding
- Color: Not required (black & white OK)
- Quality: Standard office paper (20 lb)
- Binding: Spiral binding or staples

**Estimated Cost**: $5-15 per person, one-time

---

### PRIORITY TIER 2: FREQUENTLY USED (Print Quarterly)

**Who Should Have This**: Direct service staff, supervisors

**What to Print**:
1. **Relevant Procedures** (dossiers/03_Guides_Procedure/)
   - Which to print depends on your role:
     - **Tutors**: Procedure_Asile_Complete.md (if handling asylum cases)
     - **Case Managers**: All procedures (3-4 files, ~40 pages total)
     - **Supervisors**: All procedures (~40 pages total)
   
   - Print: 1 copy per staff member
   - Binding: Spiral or folder
   - Cost: Moderate ($2-5 per copy)
   - Update: Quarterly (procedures change)

2. **Key Case Studies** (dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/)
   - Print: 2-3 cases relevant to your work
   - Binding: Folder or filing
   - Cost: Moderate
   - Update: As needed (static content)

**Printing Instructions**:
- Include table of contents with page numbers
- Use tabs or dividers for each procedure
- Double-sided to save paper/binding cost
- Consider color for procedure flowcharts (improves clarity)

**Estimated Cost**: $10-20 per person, quarterly

---

### PRIORITY TIER 3: REFERENCE (Print As Needed)

**Who Should Have This**: Various staff as needed

**What to Print**:
1. **Forms** (dossiers/02_Formulaires_CGRA/)
   - Print blank forms on-demand as needed
   - Keep a supply of commonly used forms
   - Store in labeled filing cabinet
   
2. **Letter Templates** (dossiers/06_Templates_Lettres/)
   - Print when you need to send a letter
   - Keep digital copy for easy reuse
   - Have supervisor review before sending

3. **Case Studies** (dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/)
   - Print for training purposes
   - Print when discussing similar case
   - Use for supervision or team discussion

**Printing Instructions**:
- Print only when needed (on-demand printing)
- Keep templates digital (easy to customize)
- Store forms in accessible location
- Maintain organized filing system

**Estimated Cost**: Varies, $0-5 per use

---

### PRIORITY TIER 4: REFERENCE ONLY (Keep Digital)

**What NOT to Print**:
1. **SYLLABUS_COMPLETE.pdf** (80 pages)
   - Too long for printing
   - Better for digital search and reference
   - Print only specific sections as needed

2. **Resource Directories** (dossiers/05_Ressources/)
   - Frequently updated (outdates if printed)
   - Better searchable digitally
   - Links work on screen, not on paper
   - Print only organizations you refer to frequently

3. **Full Case Studies** (dossiers/04_Cas_Pratiques/)
   - Long documents (10-20 pages each)
   - Better for digital reading
   - Print only specific cases for training

4. **INDEX & FINDER Documents**
   - Primarily for digital navigation
   - Print only if creating print reference manual

## Organization Systems

### System 1: Individual Staff Binder

**Contents**:
- SYLLABUS_COURTE.pdf (20 pages)
- Contacts_Urgence.md (laminated insert - keeps wallet copy too)
- Your role's key procedures (20-30 pages)

**Organization**:
- Divider tabs for each section

- Tab 5: Resources & Contacts

- PDF backup on computer (searchable)

**Maintenance**:
- Review quarterly (update if procedures changed)
- Replace worn pages
- Add new procedures as needed

**Contents (One Set for the Office)**:
- SYLLABUS_COURTE.pdf (3-4 copies)

**Maintenance**:

## Printing Specifications

### Paper Quality

**Standard Office Paper**:
- Weight: 20 lb (80 gsm)
- Brightness: Standard (not necessary to pay for premium)
- Cost: Lowest cost option
- Use for: Most documents

**Heavier Stock** (24 lb):
- Weight: 24 lb
- More durable for frequently-handled documents
- Cost: 10-20% more expensive
- Use for: Covers, frequently-referenced pages

**Premium Paper** (32 lb cardstock):
- Weight: Thick, durable
- Cost: Most expensive
- Use for: Emergency contact cards (with lamination)

---

### Binding Options

**Staples**:
- Cost: Free to minimal
- Durability: Low (comes apart with use)
- Time: Quick
- Use for: Short documents (5 pages or less)

**Spiral Binding**:
- Cost: $0.50-1.00 per document
- Durability: Good (opens flat, withstands use)
- Time: Requires binding machine
- Use for: Personal reference binders (20-40 pages)

**Comb Binding**:
- Cost: $0.25-0.75 per document
- Durability: Good (opens flat, plastic combs)
- Time: Requires binding machine
- Use for: Reference manuals (30-80 pages)

**Folders/Files**:
- Cost: $0.10-0.25 each
- Durability: Low-Medium (loose items)
- Time: Quick assembly
- Use for: Case studies, forms, reference sheets

**Lamination**:
- Cost: $0.25-1.00 per item
- Durability: Excellent (waterproof, long-lasting)
- Time: Requires laminator or service
- Use for: Emergency contacts, critical procedures

---

### Color Printing

**Full Color**:
- Cost: 8-10x more expensive than B&W
- Benefit: Improved readability of charts/diagrams
- Use for: Flowcharts, decision trees, visual procedures
- Not necessary for: Text-heavy documents

**Selective Color**:
- Print headers/important items in color
- Cost: 2-3x more expensive than B&W
- Benefit: Highlights important information
- Use for: Emergency contacts (red for important), procedure headers

**Recommendation**: Color for emergency contacts and flowcharts only

## Digital Alternatives

### When to Use Digital Instead of Print

**Advantages of Digital**:
- Always current (no outdated printouts)
- Searchable (find info quickly)
- Easy to share (email, shared drive)
- Secure (password-protected, backed up)
- Accessible anywhere (with device)
- No printing costs

**When Digital is Better**:
- Resource directories (frequently updated)
- Full case study library (infrequently referenced)
- SYLLABUS_COMPLETE.pdf (primarily for training)
- Forms with complex logic (digital forms work better)
- Information that changes frequently

**Implementation**:
- Ensure all staff have digital device access
- Provide on-screen reference tools
- Use cloud storage for team access
- Backup important files regularly
- Maintain digital-only versions of everything

## Accessibility Considerations

### Font Size & Readability
- Minimum 11 pt font (no smaller)
- Higher contrast (black text on white paper)
- Avoid light gray text
- Use simple, clear fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)

### For Staff with Vision Difficulties
- Offer large-print versions
- Provide digital version for screen reader use
- Ensure PDFs are searchable (OCR if needed)
- Consider font adjustability with digital versions

### For Non-Native Readers
- Check document readability level
- Use bullet points for clarity
- Minimize jargon (or define)
- Consider providing translated versions

## Tips for Cost Savings

1. **Double-sided printing**: Reduces paper by 50%
2. **Bulk printing**: Order all at once, get better rates
3. **Share materials**: One copy per team can be shared
4. **Digital-first**: Keep primary storage digital
5. **Print-on-demand**: Print forms only when needed
6. **Free lamination alternatives**: Use plastic sleeves instead
7. **Negotiate with printer**: Volume discounts for organizations

## Version Control & Updates

### When to Reprint

**Immediately**:
- Legal procedures change
- Emergency contact information changes
- Critical errors found
- New requirements implemented

**Quarterly**:
- Standard refresh (forms, procedures)
- Worn or damaged materials replaced
- Seasonal updates

**Annually**:
- Comprehensive review
- Update if major content changes
- Refresh training materials

### Managing Outdated Materials

- Clearly mark old versions as "OUTDATED"
- Remove from circulation
- Maintain one copy for reference
- Document version and date
- Destroy old materials securely

## Conclusion

**Key Principle**: Print strategically for accessibility, keep digital for everything else.

**Start With**: Essential Tier 1 materials (contacts, checklist, overview)

**Keep Digital**: Large reference materials and frequently-updated content
