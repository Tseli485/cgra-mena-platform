# CYCLE-VIE Phase 3: Ages 6-12 Years
## Elementary School & Academic Development

**Duration:** 6-12 years from arrival or ages 6-12  
**Phase Type:** Educational Focus & Psychosocial Integration  
**Key Focus:** School enrollment, academic support, psychological evaluation, family reunification intensification

---

## 1. Story & Overview

The 6-12 years phase represents the critical elementary school years where children transition from early childhood care into formal education and educational settings. This phase encompasses:

### School Enrollment & Academic Integration (Age 6-7)
- **School Selection & Registration:** Identification of appropriate primary school matching child's needs and language capability
- **Academic Assessment:** Baseline testing to determine grade level and any learning support needs
- **Language Support:** Assessment of language skills (native language vs. school language) and determination of support level
- **Enrollment Processing:** Formal registration with school administration, submission of required documents
- **Transition Planning:** Preparation of child for school environment, introduction to new setting and routines

### Academic Support & Learning Development (Age 7-10)
- **Classroom Performance Monitoring:** Regular observation of academic progress in reading, writing, mathematics
- **Tutoring & Remedial Support:** Additional instruction for children with learning gaps or specific difficulties
- **Gifted Program Identification:** Recognition and support for advanced learners with enhanced opportunities
- **Special Education Assessment:** Formal evaluation for children with identified learning disabilities or needs
- **Educational Plan Development:** Individualized education plans (IEPs) for children with specific learning needs

### Psychological Evaluation & Mental Health Support (Ages 6-12)
- **Psychological Assessment:** Comprehensive evaluation of emotional functioning and mental health status
- **Trauma-Informed Care:** Recognition and treatment of trauma-related symptoms and behaviors
- **Behavioral Support Programs:** Intervention for behavioral difficulties and emotional regulation challenges
- **Counseling & Therapy:** Individual or group therapy for emotional support and mental health
- **School Integration Support:** Assistance with social integration, peer relationships, and school adaptation

### Family Reunification Intensification (Ages 10-12)
- **Reunification Planning:** Formalized assessment of family readiness and reunification feasibility
- **Legal Process Initiation:** Beginning of formal family reunification legal procedures
- **Family Contact Intensification:** Increased supervised visitation and communication with family members
- **Preparation for Transition:** Child preparation for potential family return or permanence with current guardian
- **Multi-Disciplinary Assessment:** Team evaluation of child's emotional readiness and family circumstances

## 2. Legal Obligations for Tuteur (20+ Required Actions)

### School Registration & Educational Rights (Actions 1-5)

1. **School Enrollment & Registration**
   - Select appropriate primary school (public, private, specialized as needed)
   - Complete and submit all enrollment documentation
   - Verify school meets educational standards and regulatory requirements
   - Obtain written acceptance and enrollment confirmation
   - Register with school administration by mandatory deadline

2. **Educational Assessment & Grade Placement**
   - Request baseline educational assessment from school
   - Provide school with all available prior educational records
   - Participate in assessment conference to determine grade level placement
   - Obtain written assessment results and grade placement determination
   - File assessment documentation in child's case file

3. **Language Support & Accommodation Determination**
   - Assess child's fluency in school language vs. native language
   - Determine if language support services are needed
   - Request language assistance programs (ESL, transitional programs)
   - Document language support plan in educational records
   - Schedule regular monitoring of language development

4. **Special Education Evaluation (if indicated)**
   - Request formal evaluation if learning difficulties suspected
   - Complete referral forms and provide background information
   - Participate in evaluation meetings and conferences
   - Obtain Individual Education Plan (IEP) if special education identified
   - Ensure accommodations and services are implemented as specified

5. **School Documentation & Record Maintenance**
   - Maintain complete educational file with all school records
   - Request and file report cards and progress reports quarterly
   - Keep copies of any assessment results or test scores
   - Document all school communications in case file
   - Update educational records annually with current information

### Psychological Assessment & Mental Health Services (Actions 6-10)

6. **Initial Psychological Evaluation**
   - Arrange comprehensive psychological assessment with qualified psychologist
   - Ensure assessment includes emotional functioning, trauma screening, and behavioral evaluation
   - Provide psychologist with relevant background information and history
   - Obtain written psychological evaluation report with recommendations
   - File psychological assessment in case file and share with school if appropriate

7. **Trauma-Informed Care Assessment**
   - Complete trauma screening to identify trauma-related symptoms
   - Assess for signs of Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) or complex trauma
   - Document any identified trauma and recommended interventions
   - Coordinate trauma-informed care approach with school and caregivers
   - Update trauma assessment annually or when circumstances change

8. **Mental Health Service Coordination**
   - Identify appropriate mental health services (individual therapy, group therapy, counseling)
   - Arrange access to mental health provider if services needed
   - Ensure coordination between school, family, mental health provider
   - Document all mental health treatment and progress notes
   - Monitor and evaluate effectiveness of mental health interventions

9. **Behavioral Support Planning**
   - If behavioral difficulties identified, develop behavioral support plan
   - Coordinate with school on behavior management strategies
   - Implement consistent behavioral expectations across settings
   - Document behavioral progress and adjust plan as needed
   - Participate in school-based behavioral intervention meetings

10. **Medical Assessment for Mental Health Medications (if applicable)**
    - If psychiatric medication considered, arrange evaluation with psychiatrist
    - Obtain detailed medical assessment and medication recommendation
    - Ensure informed consent and understanding of medication use
    - Document medication prescription and monitoring schedule
    - Monitor for medication effectiveness and side effects regularly

### Family Reunification & Contact Intensification (Actions 11-16)

11. **Family Reunification Assessment & Planning**
    - Conduct comprehensive assessment of family readiness for reunification
    - Evaluate family's ability to provide safe, stable, supportive environment
    - Assess family's engagement and commitment to reunification process
    - Develop detailed reunification plan with specific objectives and timeline
    - Document assessment findings and reunification plan

12. **Legal Family Reunification Application**
    - Prepare and submit formal family reunification application to authorities
    - Complete all required legal documentation and forms
    - Provide evidence of family identification and proof of relationship
    - Request expedited review if circumstances warrant
    - Obtain written decision and timeline for reunification process

13. **Supervised Family Visitation Program**
    - Establish schedule for supervised family visits (minimum monthly, increasing)
    - Arrange supervised visitation facility or provider
    - Document all family visits including observations and interactions
    - Monitor quality of family interactions and child's response
    - Gradually increase unsupervised contact as appropriate

14. **Family Member Assessment & Support**
    - Conduct assessment of family members' capacity and willingness
    - Identify any support services needed (counseling, parenting support, economic assistance)
    - Connect family with support resources and programs
    - Document family member progress and engagement
    - Maintain regular contact and communication with family

15. **Child Preparation for Reunification**
    - Provide psychological preparation for potential family return
    - Discuss child's feelings and expectations regarding reunification
    - If reunification occurring, prepare child for transition
    - Coordinate with mental health provider on transition support
    - Document child's responses and readiness

16. **Reunification Legal Process Coordination**
    - Prepare documentation for court review (if required)
    - Coordinate with legal representatives on reunification timeline
    - Ensure all background checks and clearances completed
    - Participate in reunification hearings or review meetings
    - If reunification approved, coordinate transition plan and follow-up

### Health & Medical Monitoring (Actions 17-19)

17. **Ongoing Medical Surveillance**
    - Schedule annual comprehensive health examination with pediatrician
    - Maintain current vaccination status (booster shots as age-appropriate)
    - Monitor growth and development (height, weight, general health)
    - Address any acute medical issues promptly
    - Maintain comprehensive medical records and documentation

18. **Dental & Vision Care**
    - Arrange annual dental examination and any needed treatment
    - Schedule vision screening and eyeglass prescription if needed
    - Ensure oral hygiene instruction and dental care access
    - Document dental and vision assessment findings
    - Address any dental or vision corrections needed

19. **Mental & Physical Wellness Monitoring**
    - Monitor overall physical health and fitness
    - Assess nutritional status and dietary needs
    - Support healthy lifestyle behaviors and activity
    - Coordinate medical care with school health programs
    - Document health maintenance and preventive care

### Documentation, Compliance & Reporting (Action 20+)

20. **Regular Progress Reporting**
    - Prepare quarterly progress reports on academic and personal development
    - Include school performance, behavioral progress, social integration
    - Document psychological/mental health status and treatment progress
    - Include any family reunification developments
    - Submit reports to child protection authority per requirements

21. **Multi-Disciplinary Team Meetings**
    - Schedule and conduct MDT meetings at least semi-annually
    - Ensure participation of school, mental health, medical, social work, family
    - Review child's progress across all domains
    - Adjust care and educational plans based on assessment
    - Document all team decisions and recommendations

22. **Case File Maintenance & Documentation**
    - Maintain comprehensive, organized case file
    - File all school records, assessments, health information
    - Keep documentation of all meetings, decisions, and contacts
    - Ensure confidentiality and secure document storage
    - Make file available for authority inspection upon request

- Successful school adjustment and attendance
- Academic skill foundation building
- Emotional security in new environment
- Peer relationship development
- Health maintenance and school-based services
- Family contact initiation

- Phonics and early reading instruction
- Basic number concepts and early math
- Foundational writing and handwriting

- School enrollment complete and regular attendance
- Grade-level appropriate academic progress
- Emerging reading and math skills

- Peer friendships beginning to form
- School routine established

- Complete school enrollment and registration
- Arrange educational assessment

## 7. Academic Support Programs

### Tutoring & Remedial Services
- **One-on-One Tutoring:** Individual instruction for reading, writing, mathematics
  - Frequency: 2-3 times per week for 60-90 minutes
  - Provider: Qualified tutor or teacher
  - Cost: €200-350/month
  - Focus on closing academic gaps and skill deficits

- **Small Group Instruction:** 3-5 students with similar needs
  - Focus: Specific skill areas (phonics, math facts, comprehension)
  - Frequency: 2 times per week
  - Cost: €100-150/month per student

- **After-School Academic Programs:** School-based or community programs
  - Homework support and academic enrichment
  - Cost: €50-100/month
  - Supervised and included with school programs

### Language Support Programs
- **ESL (English as Second Language):** For non-native English speakers
  - Duration: Variable, based on proficiency level
  - Integrated with school curriculum
  - Additional pull-out sessions if needed
  - Cost: Usually included in school services

- **Transitional Programs:** Gradual transition from native language instruction
  - Native language support while learning school language
  - Gradual reduction of native language support
  - Duration: 1-3 years depending on proficiency

- **Bilingual Education:** Maintenance of native language while learning school language
  - Instruction in both languages
  - Maintaining cultural and linguistic identity
  - Available in some schools

### Gifted & Enrichment Programs
- **Advanced Curriculum:** Grade-level advancement or accelerated instruction
  - For students performing above grade level
  - Differentiated and challenging content
  - Flexible grouping and pacing

- **Enrichment Programs:** Extended learning opportunities
  - Clubs and special interest groups
  - Advanced projects and investigations
  - Cost: €100-150/month

- **Competitions & Challenges:** Academic competitions and skill challenges
  - Math competitions, science fairs, spelling bees
  - Language arts competitions
  - Develops motivation and challenge-seeking

### Special Education Services
- **Special Education Evaluation:** Formal assessment for learning disabilities
  - Comprehensive psychoeducational testing
  - Determination of eligibility for services
  - Development of IEP if eligible

- **Special Education Instruction:** Specialized teaching for identified disabilities
  - Small class sizes with specially trained teachers
  - Individualized instruction targeting specific needs
  - Frequency based on IEP recommendations

- **Related Services:** Support services for special education students
  - Speech/language therapy
  - Occupational therapy
  - Physical therapy
  - Counseling or psychological services
  - Included in IEP as needed

## 8. Psychological Support & Mental Health Services

### Mental Health Assessment & Evaluation
- **Comprehensive Psychological Evaluation:** Initial assessment of mental health
  - Emotional functioning assessment
  - Behavioral patterns and challenges
  - Academic and social functioning
  - Trauma screening and evaluation
  - Strengths and resilience assessment
  - Cost: €300-600 for full evaluation

- **Annual Reassessment:** Ongoing psychological monitoring
  - Update on current functioning
  - Effectiveness of interventions
  - Changes and progress documentation
  - Adjustment of recommendations as needed
  - Cost: €200-400 annually

### Trauma-Focused Mental Health Services
- **Trauma Screening:** Identification of trauma symptoms
  - Screening tools for PTSD and complex trauma
  - Assessment of trauma history and impact
  - Determination of need for specialized treatment

- **Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TF-CBT):**
  - Evidence-based treatment for trauma
  - Cognitive processing of traumatic memories
  - Development of coping skills
  - Usually 12-20 sessions over 3-4 months
  - Cost: €150-250/session

- **Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR):**
  - Specialized trauma treatment
  - Processing of traumatic memories through bilateral stimulation
  - Rapid processing and symptom reduction
  - Cost: €150-250/session

### Counseling & Psychotherapy Services
- **Individual Therapy:** One-on-one counseling with mental health provider
  - Weekly or bi-weekly sessions
  - Duration based on needs (typically 6-12 months minimum)
  - Various therapeutic approaches (CBT, play therapy, psychodynamic)
  - Cost: €80-150/session

- **Play Therapy:** For younger children (ages 6-9)
  - Therapeutic use of play to address emotional issues
  - Age-appropriate expression of feelings
  - Building coping skills through play
  - Typically 1-2 times per week
  - Cost: €80-150/session

- **Group Therapy/Counseling:** Small group sessions with similar-aged peers
  - Social skills development
  - Peer support and normalization
  - Specific focus areas (trauma, grief, anxiety)
  - Typically 1 time per week
  - Cost: €50-80/session

### Behavioral Support & Intervention
- **Behavioral Assessment:** Evaluation of behavior patterns
  - Baseline measurement of targeted behaviors
  - Identification of triggers and antecedents
  - Analysis of consequences and reinforcement patterns
  - Development of behavior intervention plan

- **Behavioral Support Plan:** Structured intervention for behavioral challenges
  - Clear expectations and consequences
  - Positive reinforcement strategies
  - Environmental modifications
  - School-home coordination
  - Regular progress monitoring

- **Social Skills Training:** Development of social competence
  - Peer interaction and friendship skills
  - Conflict resolution and problem-solving
  - Emotional recognition and expression
  - Group sessions or individual coaching

### Psychiatric Services (if applicable)
- **Psychiatric Evaluation:** Assessment by psychiatrist for possible medication
  - Comprehensive diagnostic evaluation
  - Assessment of symptoms and functioning
  - Determination of medication need
  - Baseline and ongoing monitoring
  - Cost: €200-400 per evaluation

- **Medication Management:** Psychiatric medication treatment
  - Prescription and ongoing monitoring
  - Evaluation of effectiveness and side effects
  - Regular follow-up appointments (monthly to quarterly)
  - Cost: €50-150 per visit

## 9. Family Reunification Process

### Reunification Assessment Phase
**Timeline: 2-3 months**

1. **Family Identification & Verification**
   - Confirm identity and relationship of family members
   - Obtain civil documents proving relationship
   - Assess family's willingness to reunify
   - Document family circumstances and living situation

2. **Family Capacity Assessment**
   - Evaluate family's ability to provide safe, stable environment
   - Assess housing, income, employment stability
   - Identify family strengths and resources
   - Assess parenting capacity and knowledge
   - Identify support needs and services required

3. **Child Readiness Assessment**
   - Psychological evaluation of child's emotional readiness
   - Assessment of attachment to current caregivers
   - Child's understanding and feelings about reunification
   - Identification of support needs during transition
   - Development of child preparation plan

4. **Documentation & Reunification Plan**
   - Written assessment findings
   - Reunification plan with timeline and steps
   - Identification of services and support
   - Clear objectives and success criteria
   - Documentation requirements

### Legal Reunification Process
**Timeline: 2-6 months (varies by jurisdiction)**

1. **Legal Application**
   - Formal reunification application to authorities
   - Submission of assessment documentation
   - Supporting evidence and family information
   - Request for legal authorization

2. **Authority Review**
   - Review of application and documentation
   - Investigation or inquiry as needed
   - Determination of reunification feasibility
   - Approval or modifications to plan

3. **Court Process (if applicable)**
   - Court review of reunification plan
   - Legal authorization or guardianship changes
   - Documentation of rights and responsibilities
   - Official approval of reunification

### Supervised Visitation Phase
**Timeline: 3-12 months (varies based on plan)**

**Initial Phase (Months 1-3):**
- Frequency: Monthly supervised visits
- Duration: 2-4 hours per visit
- Location: Supervised visitation facility
- Activities: Getting to know family in supervised setting
- Supervision: Professional supervisor present throughout
- Documentation: Detailed observation notes

**Intermediate Phase (Months 3-6):**
- Frequency: Twice monthly visits
- Duration: 4-6 hours per visit
- Location: May include community activities
- Activities: More varied, semi-supervised activities
- Supervision: Supervisor present, less intrusive
- Responsibility increase: Family takes more active role

**Later Phase (Months 6-12):**
- Frequency: Weekly or more
- Duration: Full day or overnight (in plan)
- Location: Family home or community
- Activities: Normal family activities
- Supervision: Check-ins and support, not constant supervision
- Responsibility: Family demonstrating capacity and safety

### Family Member Support & Preparation
- **Family Counseling:** Preparation of family for reunification
  - Education about child's needs and trauma history
  - Parenting support and skill building
  - Communication and conflict resolution
  - Adjustment to reunification and child's needs

- **Economic Support (if available):**
  - Financial assistance if family has economic need
  - Employment support services
  - Housing support programs
  - Basic needs assistance

- **Mental Health Services:**
  - Therapy or counseling for family members
  - Addressing family history of trauma
  - Coping with reunification process
  - Support for caregiver transition

### Child Preparation for Reunification
- **Psychological Preparation:**
  - Processing feelings about reunification
  - Discussion of expectations and concerns
  - Addressing fears or reluctance
  - Building confidence in family relationships

- **Practical Preparation:**
  - Teaching about family and how family works
  - Preparation for life changes and new environment
  - Discussion of expectations and routines
  - What to expect in family home

- **Ongoing Therapy:**
  - Continued mental health support during transition
  - Processing experiences and feelings
  - Coping skill development
  - Attachment building support

### Transition & Follow-Up
**Timeline: Based on reunification plan (typically 3-6 months after last supervised visit)**

1. **Transition Planning**
   - Preparation of family home
   - Coordination of timing and logistics
   - Final preparations and arrangements
   - Family and child final preparations

2. **Transition Support**
   - Support during transition week
   - Initial adjustment period support
   - Regular check-ins (weekly initially)
   - Crisis support availability

3. **Post-Reunification Follow-Up (6-24 months)**
   - Regular check-in visits (monthly initially, decreasing)
   - Assessment of family adjustment
   - Continued mental health support for child
   - School monitoring and support
   - Documentation of progress
   - Gradual reduction of support as family stabilizes

## 12. Phase Transition Criteria

**Transition to Phase 4 (12-18 Years) occurs when:**
- Child reaches age 12 (or equivalent developmental stage)
- All outstanding educational assessments and services in place
- Psychological evaluation and support plan established
- Family reunification decision made and in progress or finalized
- School enrollment and academic plan established
- Mental health services ongoing or determined not needed
- Individual development on track for age
- Care arrangement stabilized and long-term plan finalized
- All required documents transferred to new phase file
- Comprehensive assessment completed

**Documentation for Phase Transition:**
- Current educational assessment and school records
- Completed psychological evaluation (within past year)
- Current health and medical records
- Family reunification status and plan
- Academic plan for continuing education
- Mental health treatment plan (if applicable)
- Case summary and transition report
