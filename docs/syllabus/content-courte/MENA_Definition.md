# MENA Definition & Initial Arrival Guide

## Who is a MENA?

**MENA** stands for *Mineur Étranger Non Accompagné* (Unaccompanied Foreign Minor).

### Legal Definition
- Under 18 years old
- Outside their country of nationality or usual residence
- No parent, legal guardian, or other responsible adult present
- Entitled to special legal protections under international law

### Legal Status in Belgium
- Considered a "minor in a vulnerable situation"
- Protected under the Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC)
- Subject to Belgian child protection law and immigration law
- Must be assigned a tuteur (legal guardian) within 72 hours of identification
- Fall under jurisdiction of SPF (Service Public Fédéral) Intérieur for asylum matters

## Basic Rights

### Under the Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC)
- **Right to life and development**
- **Right to identity and family relationships**
- **Protection from harm, exploitation, abuse**
- **Right to education** (mandatory up to age 18)
- **Right to healthcare** (physical & mental)
- **Right to participation** (being heard in decisions affecting them)

### Under Belgian Law
- **Right to asylum assessment** (if claiming protection)
- **Right to a tutor** (legal guardian assigned by court)
- **Right to social assistance** (housing, food, clothing if in care)
- **Right to legal representation** (at no cost if needed)
- **Right to information** (in a language they understand)
- **Right to education** (public school enrollment)

### Asylum-Related Rights
- **Deferral of removal** until age 18 (in most cases)
- **Access to asylum procedure** if fleeing persecution
- **Access to international protection** (refugee or subsidiary protection status)
- **Temporary status** during processing (provisional residence permit)

## Initial Arrival Procedures (First 72 Hours)

### Hour 0–2: Identification & Registration
1. **Initial contact** (police, social services, or airport)
   - Verify age (ID check, dental assessment if uncertain)
   - Record name, nationality, suspected birthdate
2. **Data entry into central registry** (Federale Vreemdelingendienst - FVD)
3. **Medical check** (basic health screening)
4. **Document collection** (if any travel documents exist)

### Hour 2–24: Assessment & Care Placement
1. **Temporary shelter placement** (emergency reception center)
2. **Initial interview** (identity, background, family)
3. **Language assessment** (determine primary language/dialect)
4. **Psychological screening** (identify trauma, mental health needs)
5. **Assignment of temporary guardian** (state-appointed, provisional)

### Hour 24–72: Legal Guardianship & Orientation
1. **Court appearance** (judge confirms permanent tuteur assignment)
2. **Tutor meeting** with MENA (introduction, role explanation)
3. **Transfer to residential care facility** (group home, foster family, or center)
4. **Initial intake interview** with care staff
5. **Educational assessment** (language level, schooling history)
6. **Healthcare registration** (GP assignment, vaccination record)

### Key Deadlines
- **Tutor appointment: within 48–72 hours** (legal requirement)
- **First court hearing: within 5 working days**
- **Asylum application deadline: within 6 months** (varies by circumstance)

## Quick Reference: First Week Checklist

- [ ] Identity confirmed, registered in central system
- [ ] Temporary shelter secured
- [ ] Tutor appointed and introduced
- [ ] Birth date and nationality documented
- [ ] Basic medical screening completed
- [ ] Language(s) spoken recorded
- [ ] School enrollment application initiated
- [ ] Family contact attempted (if safe/possible)
- [ ] Information materials provided (in accessible language)
- [ ] First care plan meeting scheduled
