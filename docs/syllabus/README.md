# Syllabus & Dossier System

## Overview

This syllabus contains the complete dossier system for MENA (Mineurs Étrangers Non Accompagnés / Unaccompanied Foreign Minors) guardianship, asylum procedures, and support services in France. The system is organized into 6 interconnected folders, each serving a specific purpose in the legal and administrative framework governing MENA protection and integration.

The dossier system is designed for:
- **Tutors and Guardians**: Daily reference guides, checklists, and obligations
- **Social Workers**: Procedures, case management, and practical scenarios
- **Legal Advisors**: Forms, procedures, and communication templates
- **Government Officials**: Proper procedures, filing requirements, and timelines
- **Organizations**: Training materials, resource directories, and best practices

## Folder Structure & Navigation

### 01_Documents_Tuteur
Core documents defining the legal obligations, rights, and responsibilities of guardians caring for MENA. Essential reading for all staff involved in guardianship.

**Key Documents:**
- Checklist of daily/weekly/monthly obligations
- Legal obligations under French law (CASF)
- Financial and budgetary requirements
- Rights of MENA

**When to use:** At the start of guardianship; regular reference throughout

---

### 02_Formulaires_CGRA
Official forms and submission documents required for asylum procedures and status reporting. Covers the complete asylum application process and ongoing communication with refugee authorities.

**Key Documents:**
- Asylum application forms and procedures
- Situation reporting forms (periodic updates)
- Complaint/appeal procedures for refusals
- Age extension applications (for 18-21 year olds)

**When to use:** At each procedural milestone (application, status update, appeal, age extension)

---

### 03_Guides_Procedure
Step-by-step guides for major administrative and legal procedures affecting MENA. Each guide includes decision trees, timelines, and contact points.

**Key Documents:**
- Complete asylum procedure (from arrival to final decision)
- Family reunification and family contact procedures
- Housing access and accommodation procedures
- School enrollment and education access procedures

**When to use:** When initiating any major procedure; reference at each step

---

### 04_Cas_Pratiques
Realistic case studies and practical scenarios illustrating common situations tutors encounter. Use for staff training, scenario-based discussions, and decision-making guidance.

**Key Cases:**
- Unaccompanied minor arrival and initial assessment
- Family conflict and family communication
- Trauma recognition and mental health response
- Missing minor emergency procedures
- Employment and labor rights questions

**When to use:** Staff training, supervision meetings, scenario discussions

---

### 05_Ressources
Comprehensive directories of services, organizations, lawyers, and emergency contacts. Essential reference for accessing support and services for MENA.

**Key Resources:**
- Legal aid and lawyer directory
- NGO and civil society organizations
- Physical centers (CADA, healthcare, education)
- Emergency contact information (police, medical, crisis)
- Service hours and accessibility information

**When to use:** When seeking external services or emergency assistance

---

### 06_Templates_Lettres
Professional letter templates for official correspondence. Use for legal communication, school enrollment, family contact, and administrative matters.

**Key Templates:**
- Letters to legal counsel
- Letters to government authorities
- Letters to schools and educational institutions
- Letters to family members
- Meeting notes and follow-up documentation

**When to use:** For any official written communication

---

## How to Navigate This System

### For New Users
1. Start with **01_Documents_Tuteur** to understand core legal obligations
2. Review **03_Guides_Procedure** for the major procedures you'll manage
3. Bookmark **05_Ressources** and **06_Templates_Lettres** for frequent access
4. Study **04_Cas_Pratiques** to see realistic scenarios

### For Specific Tasks

**Receiving a new MENA:**
1. **01_Documents_Tuteur** > Checklist_Tuteur.md
2. **03_Guides_Procedure** > Procedure_Asile_Complete.md (initial steps)
3. **04_Cas_Pratiques** > Cas_1_Unaccompanied.md

**Preparing asylum application:**
1. **03_Guides_Procedure** > Procedure_Asile_Complete.md
2. **02_Formulaires_CGRA** > Demande_Asile.md
3. **06_Templates_Lettres** > Lettre_Autorite.md

**Handling family contact request:**
1. **04_Cas_Pratiques** > Cas_2_Family_Conflict.md
2. **03_Guides_Procedure** > Procedure_Famille.md
3. **06_Templates_Lettres** > Lettre_Famille.md

**Emergency (missing minor):**
1. **05_Ressources** > Contacts_Urgence.md (immediate action)

3. **01_Documents_Tuteur** > Obligations_Legales.md (reporting obligations)

## File Organization & Naming Conventions

### Naming Pattern
All files follow this convention:
- **README.md** in each folder = overview and navigation
- **Document names** = descriptive English/French labels with underscores
- **No special characters** except underscores and hyphens

Example: `Procedure_Asile_Complete.md` (not `Procedure d'Asile Complète`)

### Folder Organization

```
docs/syllabus/
├── README.md (this file)
└── dossiers/
    ├── 01_Documents_Tuteur/
    ├── 02_Formulaires_CGRA/
    ├── 03_Guides_Procedure/
    ├── 04_Cas_Pratiques/
    ├── 05_Ressources/
    └── 06_Templates_Lettres/
```

All folders include:
- README.md (explains purpose and contents)
- Topic-specific document files (4-6 per folder)
- No nested subfolders (flat structure for simplicity)

## Recommendations for Use

### Print & Distribution
- **Print policy**: Print key documents (checklists, emergency contacts, procedures) at start of each quarter
- **Language**: Provide documents in French and in the minor's language when possible
- **Lamination**: Laminate emergency contact cards for durability
- **Updates**: Review all documents annually; update as regulations change

### Staff Training
- Use **04_Cas_Pratiques** as the foundation of scenario-based training
- Have new hires read **01_Documents_Tuteur** completely
- Use **03_Guides_Procedure** as training modules for specific procedures
- Conduct monthly "case study" meetings using **04_Cas_Pratiques**

### Compliance & Auditing
- Track all actions against **01_Documents_Tuteur** checklists
- Keep copies of all communications using **06_Templates_Lettres**
- Document all procedures followed from **03_Guides_Procedure**
- Maintain records as required by **01_Documents_Tuteur** > Obligations_Legales.md

### Cross-References
Every document includes cross-references to related documents in other folders. Use these to:
- Understand the broader context of any procedure
- Find related obligations, forms, and resources
- Navigate efficiently between related topics

## Legal Framework

This dossier system is based on:
- **CASF** (Code de l'action sociale et des familles) - Child welfare law
- **CESEDA** (Code de l'entrée et du séjour des étrangers) - Immigration law
- **UNCRC** (UN Convention on Rights of Child) - International protections
- **French Constitution** - Fundamental rights and equality
- **CGRA** regulations and procedures - Refugee assessment
- **Rectorat** (Ministry of Education) - School access and integration
