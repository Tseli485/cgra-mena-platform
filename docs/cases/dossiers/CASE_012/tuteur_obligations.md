# Tuteur Obligations - Additional Complex Case - Legal, Employment & Family Intersections

## Step-by-Step Tuteur Actions


Mme. Farah Hassan's responsibilities as tuteur for Bashir M.:

1. **Comprehensive Initial Assessment and Case Planning**
   - Assess abuse, labor exploitation, medical crisis, educational status, family situation
   - Document all findings comprehensively; establish safety baseline
   - Identify immediate risks and protective needs
   - Assess Bashir's own voice, understanding, and preferences
   - Develop integrated case plan addressing all domains simultaneously
   - Set realistic goals considering adolescent stage and complexity

2. **Medical Coordination and Health Management**
   - Ensure emergency medical care; oversee infection treatment
   - Coordinate ongoing medical assessments and rehabilitation
   - Ensure occupational therapy for hand recovery
   - Monitor healing progress; assess long-term prognosis
   - Ensure regular follow-up care; prevent gaps in treatment
   - Document all medical findings for legal proceedings
   - Provide health education to Bashir and family
   - Connect to long-term health monitoring systems

3. **Psychological Support and Mental Health**
   - Refer to qualified trauma-informed psychologist
   - Ensure regular therapy sessions occur; monitor progress
   - Communicate with therapist regarding concerns and goals
   - Provide crisis support if severe symptoms emerge
   - Ensure any needed psychiatric medication is accessed and monitored
   - Support Bashir's emotional processing; validate his experience
   - Monitor for suicide/self-harm risk; take protective action if needed
   - Coordinate psychological support with other interventions

4. **Educational Assessment, Re-engagement, and Academic Support**
   - Conduct educational assessment; identify gaps and learning needs
   - Recognize that standard grade-level return is inappropriate
   - Identify appropriate educational program (vocational track)
   - Ensure enrollment in suitable education program
   - Coordinate with school regarding Bashir's needs and history
   - Monitor attendance and academic progress
   - Provide additional support (tutoring, literacy specialists) if needed
   - Support peer reintegration and social adjustment
   - Advocate for accommodations based on trauma and learning gaps

5. **Safety Assessment and Protective Planning**
   - Assess home environment for safety; identify abuse risks
   - Make sheltering decision if necessary for immediate protection
   - Coordinate with authorities regarding abuser's removal/arrest
   - Develop safety plan for Bashir; ensure clear protocols
   - Monitor safety continuously; escalate if risks emerge
   - Work with mother to strengthen her protective capacity
   - Ensure other younger siblings in household are also protected
   - Regular safety assessment; documented monitoring

6. **Family Strengthening and Relationship Support**
   - Assess family relationships and dynamics; document strengths
   - Support mother Amal's capacity to protect and care for Bashir
   - Provide mother with individual counseling and support
   - Facilitate appropriate involvement of extended family
   - Assess father Ibrahim's capacity and willingness to contribute
   - Support healthy relationships while maintaining protective focus
   - Monitor for signs of abuse recurrence or family deterioration
   - Celebrate family strengths and progress

7. **Maternal Support and Domestic Violence Response**
   - Assess mother's own trauma and wellbeing
   - Provide psychoeducation and counseling for trauma and abuse
   - Support mother's economic empowerment and job seeking
   - Ensure mother has access to mental health services if needed
   - Recognize mother's own vulnerability while ensuring child protection
   - Connect mother to domestic violence resources if abuser still present/threatening
   - Support mother's parenting capacity and confidence
   - Ensure mother is involved in decisions and planning

8. **Economic Support and Livelihood Restructuring**
   - Assess household's economic situation comprehensively
   - Assist with applications for government assistance
   - Monitor receipt of emergency assistance; ensure appropriate use
   - Support mother's employment search and job placement
   - Explore income-generating options that don't involve child labor
   - Provide economic literacy and budgeting support
   - Monitor family's economic situation; address crises quickly
   - Plan for sustainable economic stability beyond emergency assistance

9. **Legal Advocacy and Prosecution Support**
   - Coordinate with police investigation; ensure proper evidence gathering
   - Support victim (Bashir) through legal process; minimize re-traumatization
   - Prepare Bashir for testimony if needed; explain process
   - Monitor prosecution progress; follow up if cases stall
   - Ensure victim's rights are protected throughout legal proceedings
   - Advocate for appropriate consequences for perpetrators
   - Protect Bashir's confidentiality and privacy in legal process
   - Support family through legal proceedings

10. **Labor Abuse Investigation and Employer Accountability**
    - Ensure child labor violation is properly investigated
    - Coordinate with labor authorities; document work conditions
    - Support investigation into employer Mustafa's actions
    - Ensure accountability for knowing exploitation of minor
    - Monitor for employer retaliation or threats
    - Support other potential child laborers at workplace if identified
    - Advocate for workplace safety improvements
    - Ensure prosecution and consequences for labor violations

11. **Service Coordination and Multi-Sectoral Integration**
    - Establish contact with all service providers (medical, school, psychological, legal)
    - Hold regular multi-disciplinary case conferences
    - Ensure integrated approach across all domains
    - Address service gaps or conflicts between providers
    - Maintain comprehensive case documentation
    - Ensure information-sharing and coordination (with confidentiality)
    - Serve as single point of contact for family

12. **Advocacy and Rights Protection**
    - Ensure Bashir's voice and preferences are centered in decision-making
    - Advocate for his rights to education, health, protection from exploitation
    - Challenge barriers to services or discrimination
    - Ensure non-discrimination based on labor history, family situation
    - Support Bashir's development of agency and self-advocacy
    - Recognize Bashir's capabilities as older adolescent; involve him in planning

13. **Monitoring, Outcomes, and Case Transition**
    - Track indicators across health, psychological, educational, family, economic, legal domains
    - Monitor healing of injury; functional recovery
    - Monitor psychological recovery; resilience building
    - Monitor educational progress and engagement
    - Monitor family stability and safety
    - Monitor economic sustainability and reduced poverty
    - Plan transition from intensive to monitoring phase (around 12 months)
    - Establish clear re-opening criteria if situation deteriorates
    - Celebrate progress and family's resilience

## Process Timeline


**2010 (Birth)**
- Bashir born to Amal and Ibrahim
- Initial years: household relatively stable; father present in early childhood

**2012-2018 (Age 2-8)**
- Father Ibrahim's involvement declines as marriage deteriorates
- Amal works as cleaner; extended family provides some support
- Bashir attends primary school; performance adequate
- Age 6: Father remarries; contact with Bashir becomes minimal
- Karim (mother's second husband) joins household; initially relationship neutral

**2018-2020 (Age 8-10)**
- Karim's employment becomes irregular
- Two half-siblings born (ages 6 and 4 by 2025)
- Household finances increasingly stressed
- Bashir's school attendance remains consistent
- Extended family support begins to diminish

**2021-2022 (Age 11-12)**
- Amal loses cleaning job; remains unemployed for extended period
- Karim's income insufficient for household; tensions increase
- First signs of family stress: increased marital conflict
- Karim's behavior becomes increasingly controlling and aggressive
- Bashir's school attendance begins to drop (89% to 76%)

**2023 (Age 13)**
- Household falls into poverty; basic needs not consistently met
- Amal desperately seeks work; takes occasional informal jobs
- Bashir completely leaves school; no longer attends
- Mustafa (distant relative) offers Bashir work in auto repair shop
- Bashir begins working 10-12 hours daily; earns $3-5 per day
- Work involves dangerous machinery, toxic chemicals, no safety equipment
- Karim becomes verbally abusive; first incident of physical punishment

**2024 (Age 14)**
- Bashir continues informal labor; income becomes critical to family survival
- Work conditions remain hazardous; safety concerns raised periodically by Mustafa's employees (but not acted upon)
- Karim's abuse escalates; physical punishment becomes regular occurrence
- Amal attempts to intervene; relationship becomes more strained
- Bashir shows signs of depression and anxiety
- August: Bashir sustains serious hand injury (laceration from machinery)
- Injury initially treated with home remedies; no medical care accessed
- Infection develops (within 1 week); fever, pus, swelling evident
- Karim blames Bashir; physical argument ensues; Karim hits Bashir
- Systemic infection apparent; Bashir becomes seriously ill (fever, weakness, delirium)

**2025 (January)**

- Police interview reveals child labor, abuse, medical neglect

**2025 (January-February)**

- Educational assessment: Bashir 3-4 grades behind; literacy and numeracy gaps significant
