# CYCLE-VIE: MENA Lifecycle Phases Framework

## Overview

The CYCLE-VIE Phase Framework is a comprehensive governance and lifecycle management system designed for MENA (Middle East and North Africa) regions. It provides a structured approach to managing individuals' lifecycle phases from birth through adolescence (0-18 years), with clear definitions of legal obligations, financial requirements, rights, and necessary documentation at each stage.

## Framework Principles

1. **Age-Segmented Structure**: Four primary age groups with 20+ distinct phases
2. **Legal Compliance**: Aligned with Islamic law principles and MENA national regulations
3. **Financial Transparency**: Clear cost and resource allocation per phase
4. **Rights Protection**: Documented safeguards at each stage
5. **Documentation Trail**: Comprehensive record-keeping requirements
6. **Holistic Coverage**: Health, education, legal, and social dimensions

---

## Age Groups Overview

### Age Group 1: Infancy (0-2 years)
**Duration**: 24 months  
**Primary Focus**: Survival, health, bonding, foundational care  
**Key Phases**: 5 phases  
**Stakeholders**: Parents, midwives, pediatricians, guardians

### Age Group 2: Early Childhood (2-6 years)
**Duration**: 48 months  
**Primary Focus**: Development, language, socialization, preschool preparation  
**Key Phases**: 6 phases  
**Stakeholders**: Parents, caregivers, educators, health workers

### Age Group 3: Middle Childhood (6-12 years)
**Duration**: 72 months  
**Primary Focus**: Education, skill development, peer relationships, independence  
**Key Phases**: 5 phases  
**Stakeholders**: Parents, teachers, school administrators, mentors

### Age Group 4: Adolescence (12-18 years)
**Duration**: 72 months  
**Primary Focus**: Identity formation, vocational exploration, legal rights, transition to adulthood  
**Key Phases**: 6 phases  
**Stakeholders**: Parents, teachers, counselors, employers, legal guardians

## Detailed Phase Breakdown

### AGE GROUP 1: INFANCY (0-2 Years)

#### Phase 1.1: Birth & Registration (0-1 month)
**Legal Obligations:**
- Birth registration with civil authorities (within 30-60 days per MENA standards)
- Religious naming ceremony (Aqiqah) within 7 days
- Notification to family and community

**Financial Requirements:**
- Hospital/midwife fees: $200-1,500 USD
- Registration fees: $20-100 USD
- Celebration/ceremony costs: $100-500 USD

**Rights & Protections:**
- Right to nationality and birth certificate
- Right to medical care
- Right to name and identity
- Inheritance rights (registered with estate authorities)

**Resources Required:**
- Valid parental ID documents
- Marriage certificate of parents
- Hospital discharge papers
- Witness attestations (2-4 witnesses depending on jurisdiction)

**Documents Generated:**
- Birth certificate (civil)
- Islamic birth registry (if applicable)
- Hospital discharge summary
- Vaccination record initiation

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Hospital birth completed
- [ ] Registration application submitted
- [ ] Naming ceremony conducted
- [ ] Initial vaccinations scheduled
- [ ] Birth certificate received
- [ ] Health insurance enrolled

## Cross-Phase Requirements

### Universal Documentation Standards
- All documents must be in Arabic and English (or regional languages)
- Digital and physical copies maintained for 18+ years
- Privacy and confidentiality strictly enforced
- Regular backup and security protocols

### Financial Tracking
- Transparent cost allocation across all phases
- Subsidies and financial assistance documented
- Payment plans and deferrals available for low-income families
- Cost-benefit analysis maintained

### Quality Assurance
- Regular audits and compliance checks per phase
- Stakeholder feedback mechanisms
- Continuous improvement protocols
- Escalation procedures for concerns

### Accessibility Requirements
- Materials available in multiple formats (print, digital, audio, video)
- Translation services for non-Arabic speakers
- Physical accessibility for persons with disabilities
- Technology access for digital services

## Implementation Notes

1. **Regional Customization**: This framework should be adapted per MENA country legal requirements
2. **Cultural Sensitivity**: All phases respect Islamic principles and local customs
3. **Family-Centered**: Emphasis on parental involvement and family unity
4. **Continuous Support**: Services available at every phase without gaps
5. **Documentation**: Complete records trail for legal protection and future reference
