# CYCLE-VIE Phase 1: Ages 0-2 Years
## Arrival to Early Childhood Care & Development

**Duration:** 0-24 months from arrival  
**Phase Type:** Foundational Care & Stabilization  
**Key Focus:** Medical assessment, legal documentation, initial shelter placement, and developmental care

---

## 1. Story & Overview

The 0-2 years phase represents the critical initial period from a child's arrival into CGRA care through the first two years of life. This phase encompasses:

### Arrival Assessment (Week 1)
- **First Contact & Triage:** Child arrives at CGRA intake center, physical and psychological condition assessed
- **Initial Health Screening:** Vital signs, visible injuries, signs of malnutrition or disease documented
- **Legal Status Determination:** Verification of age, nationality status, and existing documentation
- **Risk Assessment:** Identification of medical, psychological, or safeguarding concerns requiring immediate intervention

### Medical Screening (Weeks 2-4)
- **Comprehensive Health Examination:** Pediatric evaluation including growth metrics, developmental milestones
- **Laboratory Testing:** Blood work, tuberculosis screening, infectious disease testing per CGRA protocols
- **Vaccination Assessment:** Determining vaccination history and scheduling immunizations
- **Specialist Referrals:** Pediatrician, nutritionist, and psychologist evaluations as needed

### Initial Shelter Placement (Weeks 4-12)
- **Placement in Accredited Facility:** Transition to licensed infant/toddler care facility with trained caregivers
- **Attachment Formation:** Consistent caregiver assignment to facilitate emotional security and bonding
- **Daily Care Routine Establishment:** Sleep schedule, feeding protocol, hygiene routine, and developmental stimulation
- **Family Contact Protocol:** Initial attempts to locate and contact biological family/guardians

## 2. Legal Obligations for Tuteur (16+ Required Actions)

### Documentation & Identity (Actions 1-4)
1. **Identity Document Issuance**
   - Apply for national identity number/card for child within 30 days of arrival
   - Coordinate with civil registry office
   - Obtain temporary identification document if permanent unavailable

2. **Birth Certificate Processing**
   - Secure or request certified copy from country of origin
   - If unavailable, initiate legal birth certificate establishment procedure
   - File attestation with local civil authority

3. **Medical Documentation Registry**
   - Create and maintain individual medical file containing:
     - Initial health assessment reports
     - Vaccination records and immunization schedule
     - Pediatric examination findings
     - Any ongoing medical conditions or prescriptions
     - Growth charts and developmental milestones

4. **Asylum/Legal Status Application**
   - Submit asylum application within legal timeframe (typically 90 days)
   - Document family situation and reason for displacement
   - Coordinate with immigration authorities
   - Maintain proof of application submission

### Family Contact & Social Documentation (Actions 5-8)
5. **Family Tracing Initiation**
   - Engage family tracing services within 6 weeks of arrival
   - Document any information child has about family location/contacts
   - Maintain contact logs with tracing organization

6. **Social Situation Assessment**
   - Complete detailed social history documentation
   - Record information about family structure, prior living conditions
   - Document reasons for separation from family
   - Identify any siblings, extended family members

7. **Case File Creation & Maintenance**
   - Establish secure, confidential case file system
   - Include intake assessments, medical records, social reports
   - Maintain regular updated entries documenting progress
   - Ensure GDPR/data protection compliance

8. **Guardian/Tuteur Appointment Documentation**
   - Official court appointment of tuteur as legal guardian
   - Registration with family court and child protection services
   - Obtain guardianship certificate
   - Document all legal responsibilities and authority limits

### Care & Welfare Planning (Actions 9-12)
9. **Individual Care Plan Development**
   - Create comprehensive 12-month care plan including:
     - Health objectives and medical appointments
     - Developmental milestones and educational goals
     - Emotional and social needs
     - Family reunification objectives (if applicable)

10. **Regular Medical Appointments Scheduling**
    - Schedule pediatric check-ups every 3 months
    - Arrange vaccination clinic visits per schedule
    - Book specialist appointments (nutrition, psychology, etc.)
    - Maintain appointment calendar and follow-up system

11. **Facility & Caregiver Selection**
    - Evaluate accredited infant/toddler care facilities
    - Ensure facility meets regulatory standards
    - Select trained caregivers with infant care certification
    - Verify background checks and references

12. **Financial Account Opening**
    - Open dedicated savings account for child's benefits/allowances
    - Register for government family allowances and subsidies
    - Document all financial transactions
    - Maintain separate accounting from tuteur personal funds

### Welfare Monitoring & Reporting (Actions 13-16)
13. **Regular Welfare Monitoring**
    - Conduct monthly in-person visits to child in care facility
    - Observe health, hygiene, emotional state, and care quality
    - Document observations in case file
    - Address any concerns with facility management immediately

14. **Quarterly Progress Reports**
    - Prepare detailed quarterly reports on child's progress
    - Include health metrics, developmental achievements, behavioral observations
    - Document any incidents or concerns
    - Submit to child protection authority per local requirements

15. **Multi-Disciplinary Team Meetings**
    - Coordinate meetings with pediatrician, social worker, psychologist
    - Review case every 6 months or as needed
    - Adjust care plan based on assessment outcomes
    - Document all team decisions and recommendations

16. **Legal Compliance Documentation**
    - Maintain records proving completion of all legal obligations
    - Keep copies of all applications, approvals, and certifications
    - Prepare annual compliance reports for authorities
    - Ensure all documents are current and accessible during inspections

## 6. Age-Specific Phases Within 0-2 Years

### Phase 1.1: Initial Arrival (0-3 Months)
**Focus:** Stabilization, Assessment, Initial Care

**Key Activities:**
- Initial health screening and medical assessment
- Determination of age and baseline development
- Assessment of any acute medical needs
- Initial attachment forming with primary caregiver
- Family location and contact initiation
- Legal documentation processing begins

**Developmental Characteristics:**
- Newborn to 3-month developmental stage
- Potential malnutrition or developmental delays from prior circumstances
- Adaptation to new environment and caregiver
- Sleep/wake pattern establishment
- Feeding routine development

**Care Priorities:**
- Adequate nutrition and growth monitoring
- Temperature regulation and comfort
- Infection control and hygiene
- Medical monitoring and treatment of identified conditions
- Creating safe, predictable environment

**Expected Outcomes by Week 12:**
- Stable vital signs and feeding pattern
- Growth trajectory established (weight gain or stabilization)
- Initial vaccinations completed or scheduled
- Primary caregiver attachment forming
- Medical conditions identified and treatment plan active

**Tuteur Actions:**
- Conduct comprehensive intake assessment
- Arrange pediatric and specialist evaluations
- Initiate family tracing services
- Apply for identification documents and legal status
- Open financial accounts and register for benefits
- Schedule first care facility placement

---

### Phase 1.2: Medical Assessment & Initial Treatment (3-6 Months)
**Focus:** Comprehensive Health Evaluation, Baseline Establishment

**Key Activities:**
- Complete laboratory and disease screening results reviewed
- Vaccination schedule in progress
- Specialist evaluations (pediatrician, psychologist, nutritionist)
- Assessment of development relative to age
- Ongoing family tracing with potential contact
- Facility placement optimization

**Developmental Characteristics:**
- 3-6 month typical development (or adjusted based on age/prior conditions)
- Increasing awareness and responsiveness
- Development of social smile and vocalizations
- Motor skill progression (rolling, reaching, grasping)
- Potential developmental delay assessment and intervention

**Medical Priorities:**
- Completion of initial disease screening
- Vaccination schedule continuation
- Assessment and treatment of nutritional deficiencies
- Monitoring of catch-up growth if needed
- Identification of chronic conditions requiring ongoing management

**Care Adjustments:**
- Facility programming adjusted based on assessments
- Specialized diet implementation if indicated
- Therapeutic interventions begun (PT, OT, speech if needed)
- Environmental modifications based on specific needs

**Expected Outcomes by Month 6:**
- All initial medical screening complete
- Vaccination schedule half-complete
- Growth trajectory normalized or improving
- Developmental assessment completed
- Family contact established or reunification plan developed
- Care facility and caregiver relationship stable

**Tuteur Actions:**
- Review and act on all specialist recommendations
- Ensure vaccination compliance
- Monitor facility care quality and child progress
- Follow up on family tracing status
- Prepare first quarterly progress report
- Schedule 6-month multi-disciplinary team meeting

---

### Phase 1.3: Initial Shelter & Care Optimization (6-12 Months)
**Focus:** Routine Establishment, Development Support, Family Planning

**Key Activities:**
- Continued medical follow-up and routine healthcare
- Introduction of age-appropriate developmental activities
- Facility routine fully established
- Family reunification assessment (if applicable)
- Transition to solid foods (typically 6 months)
- Language and cognitive stimulation

**Developmental Characteristics:**
- 6-12 month development (or adjusted for actual age)
- Increased motor skills (sitting, crawling, standing progression)
- Improved communication (babbling to words)
- Emerging sense of object permanence and cause/effect
- Stranger anxiety and preference for primary caregiver
- Increased interest in environment and exploration

**Care Routine Established:**
- Regular sleep/wake schedule
- Feeding pattern with introduction of solids
- Daily caregiver interaction patterns
- Regular developmental activity participation
- Consistent medical appointment follow-up
- Family visitation schedule (if applicable)

**Education & Development:**
- Language stimulation with multilingual elements as applicable
- Sensory play and motor development activities
- Music classes or similar developmental programming
- Interaction with peers in facility setting
- Cognitive stimulation through play

**Expected Outcomes by Month 12:**
- Vaccinations completed or on final doses
- Growth and development within expected range
- First words or language development evident
- Strong attachment to primary caregiver
- Independent mobility (crawling/standing/early walking)
- Family reunification plan finalized or permanence plan established
- Care routine predictable and child thriving

**Tuteur Actions:**
- Ensure continued medical appointment compliance
- Assess developmental progress against milestones
- Monitor and support caregiver-child attachment
- Coordinate family visits if reunification ongoing
- Prepare annual comprehensive assessment
- Plan transition to Phase 1.4 (12-24 months care)

---

### Phase 1.4: Toddler Care & Development (12-24 Months)
**Focus:** Independence Building, Language Development, Future Planning

**Key Activities:**
- Toddler-specific developmental activities
- Speech and language development support
- Motor skill refinement (walking, climbing, coordination)
- Self-care skill introduction (eating utensils, toileting awareness)
- Continued medical surveillance and health maintenance
- Development of social skills and peer interaction
- Preparation for next phase (transition planning)

**Developmental Characteristics:**
- 12-24 month toddler stage (or adjusted for actual age)
- Rapid language development (50+ words by 24 months)
- Independent walking and running
- Increased independence and autonomy assertions
- Play with purpose and imagination
- Recognition of self in mirror/photos
- Emerging behavioral management needs

**Toddler-Specific Care:**
- Transition from formula to milk and varied foods
- Increased physical activity accommodation
- Introduction of basic discipline and boundary setting
- Enhanced safety measures (child-proofing)
- Toddler-sized furniture and activities
- Shorter attention span activity planning
- Increased outdoor play and exploration

**Educational Programming:**
- Toddler music and movement classes
- Story time and language development
- Pretend play and social interaction
- Fine motor activities (coloring, stacking, manipulatives)
- Gross motor activities (climbing, running, jumping)
- Peer interaction encouragement

**Family & Future Planning:**
- Finalization of family reunification (if ongoing)
- Or establishment of permanent care arrangement
- Preparation for next phase transition (24+ months)
- Planning for eventual school enrollment
- Assessment of any ongoing special needs
- Development of long-term care plan

**Expected Outcomes by Month 24:**
- Child walking, running, climbing independently
- Vocabulary of 50+ words with emerging phrases
- Ability to follow simple instructions
- Increased independence in self-care
- Strong attachments and social skills
- Medical supervision current and compliant
- Clear plan for next phase (family, foster care, etc.)
- Thriving in current care setting

**Tuteur Actions:**
- Conduct toddler developmental assessment
- Monitor speech and language progression
- Support independence building activities
- Plan and conduct family transition (reunification or permanence)
- Complete 24-month comprehensive review
- Establish long-term care and educational plan
- Prepare documentation for next phase transition

## 9. Phase Transition Criteria

**Transition to Phase 2 (2-5 Years) occurs when:**
- Child reaches 24 months chronological age or equivalent developmental stage
- All outstanding medical assessments and treatments completed
- Family reunification decided and either active or ruled out
- Individual development on track for age
- Care arrangement stabilized and ongoing
- Comprehensive 24-month assessment completed
- Guardianship and legal status formalized
- Long-term care plan established

**Documentation for Phase Transition:**
- Completed 24-month health assessment
- Current vaccination records
