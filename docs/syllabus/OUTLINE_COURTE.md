## PAGE 3: Qui est un MENA? / What is a MENA?
**Format**: Definition card with key facts

**Content**:
- **Definition**: "Mentor-Éducateur-Non-Agréé - A non-accredited tutor operating within legal framework"
- **Key Characteristics** (bullet list):
  - Provides supervised mentoring/tutoring (not formal education)
  - Operates outside accredited institutions
  - Must comply with regional legal requirements
  - Works with children aged 0-18 (age-specific obligations vary)
  - Functions independently or through coordinating programs
- **Legal Status**: 
  - Not an educator by certification
  - Subject to child protection laws
  - Responsible for duty of care
  - Must maintain confidentiality
- **Distinction from**:
  - Accredited educators (school teachers)
  - Childcare workers (different license)
  - Parents/guardians (different legal standing)
- **Your Role in 3 Words**: Trusted. Responsible. Accountable.

**Page 4 - Core Responsibilities**:

  - Maintain professional boundaries
  - Document sessions and progress
  - Manage confidentiality responsibly
  - Communicate with parents/guardians regularly
- **Administrative Role**:
  - Keep required records
  - Maintain compliance documentation

**Format**: Structured checklist of legal requirements

**Content**:
- **You MUST**:
  - Comply with child protection laws in your region
  - Report suspected abuse/neglect
  - Maintain child safety and confidentiality
  - Keep required records for [X years]

  - Respect working hours/labor regulations (if applicable)

## PAGE 12: Step-by-Step Procedures (2 pages)
**Format**: Quick procedural flowcharts and checklists

**Page 12 - Procedure 1: Starting a New Tutoring Relationship**

**Step 1: Initial Contact with Parent/Guardian**
- [ ] Introduce yourself and qualifications (be honest)
- [ ] Discuss child's needs, learning style, and goals
- [ ] Clarify your expertise and any limitations
- [ ] Explain tutoring approach and methods
- [ ] Discuss frequency, duration, and location
- [ ] Answer questions about confidentiality and communication

**Step 2: Contract/Agreement (informal or formal)**
- [ ] Agree on fee and payment method
- [ ] Clarify cancellation policy
- [ ] Specify confidentiality and privacy expectations
- [ ] Document start date and anticipated duration
- [ ] Confirm emergency contact information
- [ ] Get written consent for communication method (calls, texts, email)

**Step 3: First Session - Meet the Child**
- [ ] Greet warmly; establish safety and comfort
- [ ] Assess current level and learning style
- [ ] Explain what to expect from tutoring
- [ ] Start with lower-stakes activity to build rapport
- [ ] Document initial observations
- [ ] Clarify with child what topics they'll work on

**Step 4: Establish Routine**
- [ ] Set consistent meeting time/place
- [ ] Create simple progress tracking system
- [ ] Establish communication plan with parent (weekly updates?)
- [ ] Review goals with parent after first 2-3 sessions
- [ ] Document all sessions briefly in writing

---

**Page 13 - Procedure 2: Reporting Suspected Abuse/Neglect**

**IMMEDIATE STEPS (If child in danger)**:
- [ ] Remove child from danger immediately
- [ ] Call emergency services (police/ambulance)
- [ ] Provide first aid if needed
- [ ] Inform child you're helping them
- [ ] Do NOT interrogate child about abuse
- [ ] Do NOT ask leading questions

**WITHIN 24 HOURS (After immediate safety secured)**:
- [ ] Report to Child Protective Services or equivalent
  - Contact: [Local Authority Name, Phone, Website]
  - Have ready: Child's name/age, parent/guardian info, specific observations
- [ ] Document exactly what you observed:
  - Date, time, description of injuries/behavior
  - Child's exact words (if any disclosure)
  - Context of observation
  - Your contact information for follow-up
- [ ] Keep a copy of your report for records
- [ ] Do NOT contact the parent/guardian first (CPS will handle)

**FOLLOW-UP**:
- [ ] Maintain child safety in subsequent sessions
- [ ] Do not discuss investigation with child
- [ ] Cooperate with CPS if they contact you
- [ ] Maintain confidentiality (don't tell other families)
- [ ] Document any further concerns
- [ ] Seek guidance from supervisor/coordinator if overwhelmed

**CRITICAL**: You are mandated reporter. Reporting is your legal obligation, not optional.

## PAGE 18: Key Legal Definitions (Glossary Quick Reference)
**Format**: Concise definitions for legal/technical terms used throughout guide

**Content**:

- **Abuse**: Physical harm, sexual abuse, emotional harm caused by caregiver
- **Neglect**: Failure to provide food, shelter, medical care, supervision, education
- **Duty of Care**: Legal obligation to ensure child's safety and wellbeing
- **Confidentiality**: Keeping information about child/family private (except required reporting)
- **Mandatory Reporter**: Person required by law to report suspected abuse
- **Child Protective Services**: Government agency investigating abuse/neglect reports
- **Self-Employed**: Work for yourself, responsible for taxes and insurance
- **Professional Boundaries**: Appropriate distance/relationship between tutor and child/family
- **Conflict of Interest**: Situation where personal/financial interests could bias professional judgment
- **Informed Consent**: Parent/guardian understanding and agreeing to tutoring approach
- **IEP (Individualized Education Plan)**: Legal document outlining special education services
- **Developmental Milestone**: Expected skill/ability at certain age
- **Learning Disability**: Neurological difference affecting learning despite adequate instruction
- **Safeguarding**: Preventive measures to ensure child safety and wellbeing

## PAGE 19: Quick Decision Tree - "What Should I Do?"
**Format**: Visual flowcharts for common scenarios

**Content**:

**Scenario 1: Child tells you they were hurt**
```
Child discloses harm
    ↓
Is child in immediate danger?
    ├─ YES → Call 911 immediately
    └─ NO → Listen without judgment
              ↓
              Don't interrogate; just listen
              ↓
              Report to CPS within 24 hours
              ↓
              Document: child's exact words, date, time, context
              ↓
              Inform child you had to tell someone to help
              ↓
              Cooperate with CPS investigation
```

**Scenario 2: You notice signs of abuse/neglect**
```
Observe suspicious signs (bruising, neglect, fear)
    ↓
Document: date, time, exact observations
    ↓
Is it severe/life-threatening?
    ├─ YES → Call 911
    └─ NO → Report to CPS within 24 hours
              ↓
              Do NOT contact parent first
              ↓
              Have documentation ready
              ↓
              Keep matter confidential
              ↓
              Continue tutoring; monitor situation
```

**Scenario 3: Parent asks you to keep secret from other parent**
```
Parent requests confidentiality from other parent
    ↓
Is it about abuse or child safety?
    ├─ YES → You cannot honor confidentiality
              Explain you must report abuse
    └─ NO → You can respect discretion
              But clarify your limits: you may need to share with other parent
              if it affects child's safety/tutoring
              
              Document the request
              Inform coordinator/supervisor if applicable
```

**Scenario 4: Child's behavior concerns you (aggression, fear, withdrawal)**
```
Child shows behavioral changes
    ↓
Is there immediate danger?
    ├─ YES → Intervene; ensure safety
    └─ NO → Observe pattern over 2-3 sessions
              ↓
              Document specific behaviors, triggers, frequency
              ↓
              Could it be:
              ├─ Normal developmental? → Monitor; communicate with parent
              ├─ Learning difficulty? → Assess; recommend evaluation
              ├─ Stress/anxiety? → Communicate with parent; suggest support
              └─ Abuse-related? → Suspect abuse; report to CPS
```
