# Documents Checklist - Mixed Vulnerabilities 2 - Trafficking, Psychological Trauma & Educational Delay

## Required Documents & Status

Based on the Solutions Implemented section:


1. **Safe Recovery and Emergency Medical Intervention**
   - Police-coordinated recovery from trafficking situation; safe transport
   - Medical assessment at specialized facility; comprehensive health evaluation
   - Emergency treatment for malnutrition: gradual refeeding protocol (prevent refeeding syndrome)
   - Wound care and infection treatment; antibiotic therapy as needed
   - Vaccination and immunization assessment; completion of routine vaccines
   - Nutritional rehabilitation program: specialized food, micronutrient supplementation
   - Weekly monitoring of weight, height, vital signs; health tracking

2. **Psychological Trauma Support and Recovery**
   - Initial psychosocial first aid; establishment of safety and basic trust
   - Referral to trauma-informed child psychologist specializing in trafficking survivors
   - Play-based therapy; trauma-focused cognitive behavioral therapy (TF-CBT) adapted for age
   - Techniques for managing dissociation, hypervigilance, fear responses
   - Graded exposure to re-traumatization; carefully paced relationship rebuilding
   - Sessions with therapist twice weekly (intensive); ongoing monitoring
   - Coordination with mother's mental health care; family trauma support

3. **Educational Assessment and Accelerated Learning Program**
   - Comprehensive developmental and educational assessment; baseline established
   - Recognition of profound gaps in language, literacy, numeracy
   - Individualized education plan with accelerated learning goals
   - Initial non-formal education program (2 hours/day): language development focus
   - Mother tongue literacy; French language instruction
   - Transition to formal Grade 1 enrollment with additional resource support (pull-out instruction, peer buddy)
   - School-based trauma-informed support; liaison with tuteur
   - Home-based learning support; practice activities for mother to reinforce

4. **Family Reunification Assessment and Phased Reintegration**
   - Assessment of mother's mental health status and treatment needs
   - Evaluation of mother's capacity for childcare; identification of needed supports
   - Initial supervised visits between mother and Oumou; psychological preparation
   - Psychoeducation for mother regarding Oumou's trauma and expected behaviors
   - Gradual increase in contact frequency and duration
   - Planning for full-time reunification; safety planning for home environment
   - Ongoing mother-child counseling; attachment building support

5. **Maternal Mental Health Treatment and Caregiver Support**
   - Psychiatric evaluation of Aïssatou; medication management for schizophrenia
   - Mental health treatment continuity; monthly medication monitoring
   - Psychoeducation regarding mental illness, medication, and parenting
   - Parenting support program focused on trauma-informed caregiving
   - Coping strategies for managing symptoms while caring for child
   - Peer support group for mothers with mental illness
   - Crisis support system; emergency mental health access

6. **Economic Support and Housing Stability**
   - Comprehensive economic needs assessment
   - Application for government social assistance; emergency housing support
   - Referral to housing program; identification of suitable living space
   - Basic furnishing and supplies for apartment; transition-in support
   - Exploration of income-generating activities for mother
   - Microfinance or small business support; vocational assessment
   - Social safety net enrollment; ongoing assistance coordination
   - Financial literacy and budgeting support for mother

7. **Re-trafficking Prevention and Protection Planning**
   - Risk assessment for trafficking recurrence; protective factors identified
   - Community awareness regarding Oumou's vulnerabilities and support needs
   - Training for mother on trafficking risks, warning signs, protective practices
   - Safe communication with school and community regarding protection needs
   - Networking with local authorities; coordination on prevention
   - Regular monitoring of Oumou's safety, peer relationships, and activities
   - Crisis protocol if re-trafficking concerns emerge

8. **Legal and Documentation Support**
   - Birth certificate verification and registration (if needed)
   - Family law consultation regarding guardianship and custody
   - Trafficking crime documentation and reporting to law enforcement
   - Victim support services through trafficking prosecution process
   - Protection order or legal safeguards if needed
   - Documentation of case history for future reference and protection
