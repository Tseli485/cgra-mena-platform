# 04_Cas_Pratiques

## Overview
This folder contains realistic case studies and practical scenarios that tutors, social workers, and administrators may encounter when working with MENA. Each case walks through a situation step-by-step, highlighting relevant procedures, obligations, and decision points.

## Contents

### Cas_1_Unaccompanied.md
Case study: A 16-year-old arrives at the border unaccompanied with minimal documentation. Covers:
- Initial assessment and security checks
- Guardianship assignment procedures
- Asylum application preparation
- Identity verification challenges
- Educational and housing placement
- First-month compliance checklist

### Cas_2_Family_Conflict.md
Case study: A 17-year-old in guardianship discovers family member in Europe and wants contact; family relationship is complicated. Covers:
- Assessing the request for contact
- Family safety and welfare concerns
- Procedure for family communication
- Legal implications of family reunification
- Conflict resolution approaches
- Documentation of conversations

### Cas_3_Trauma.md
Case study: A 15-year-old exhibits signs of trauma, including mental health symptoms and behavioral issues. Covers:
- Recognizing trauma indicators
- Mandatory reporting obligations
- Healthcare access and referrals
- Psychological support procedures
- Accommodations in education
- Tutor's role in trauma-informed care

### Cas_4_Runaway.md
Case study: A minor in guardianship goes missing from placement. Covers:
- Immediate actions and reporting
- Search and location procedures
- Police and child protection involvement
- Communication with asylum authorities
- Documentation of missing period
- Reintegration and risk assessment

### Cas_5_Work_Situation.md
Case study: A 17-year-old expresses desire to work; questions arise about legality and labor protections. Covers:
- Legal work restrictions for asylum seekers
- Work permit eligibility and procedures
- Safe labor practices and exploitation prevention
- Tax and social security implications
- Impact on asylum application
- Rights to fair wages and conditions

## When to Use

- **All cases**: During staff training and scenario-based discussions
- **Cas_1_Unaccompanied.md**: Training new intake coordinators
- **Cas_2_Family_Conflict.md**: Training social workers on family mediation
- **Cas_3_Trauma.md**: Healthcare and education staff orientation
- **Cas_4_Runaway.md**: Emergency response team training
- **Cas_5_Work_Situation.md**: Discussing work and employment rights

## How to Print/Distribute

- Print all cases as a handbook for staff training
- Use individual cases in role-play training scenarios
- Share relevant cases with contractors and partner organizations
- Adapt cases to local contexts and regulations
- Use as discussion prompts in supervision meetings
