# Legal Analysis - Severe Trauma and PTSD - Congolese Minor

## Legal Framework Applied


| Law/Regulation | How It Applies | Responsible Party | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC, 1989) | Karim has right to protection, recovery, and family reunification | UN Organizations + DRC Services | Implemented |
| Convention on the Worst Forms of Child Labor (ILO) | Protects against forced recruitment into armed groups | International Law + DRC Services | Enforced |
| UN Optional Protocol on Children in Armed Conflict | Prohibits use of children in warfare; provides demobilization support | UNICEF + DRC Government | Applied |
| DRC Child Protection Law | Mandates protection of vulnerable children; family reunification priority | Ministry of Social Affairs | Applied |
| Medical Ethics for Child Trauma | Establishes informed consent, trauma-informed care standards | Healthcare Providers | Implemented |
| International Humanitarian Law (Geneva Conventions) | Protects kidnapped children; mandates humane treatment during repatriation | Red Cross/ICRC | Applied |
| Educational Rights | Child has right to education; remedial schooling should be available | Ministry of Education | To Be Implemented |
| Mental Health Treatment Standards | Trauma-focused therapy, psychiatric care, medication as appropriate | WHO Guidelines + Providers | Implemented |


## Problems Identified


| Problem | Description | Severity | Domain | Urgency |
|---------|-------------|----------|--------|---------|
| Complex PTSD | Kidnapping, abuse, violence, deprivation; development-altering trauma | Critical | Health/Medical | Critical |
| Sexual Abuse History | Trauma with sexual component; shame, fear, trust disruption | Critical | Health/Medical | Critical |
| Severe Malnutrition | 6 months deprivation; stunted growth; metabolic complications | High | Health/Medical | Critical |
| Attachment Disruption | Separation from family during critical developmental period (age 9) | High | Family/Reunion | Critical |
| Communication Breakdown | Selective mutism; vocabulary loss; unable to articulate experience | High | Health/Medical | High |
| Sleep Disorders | Nightmares, night terrors, insomnia; no rest restoration | High | Health/Medical | Critical |
| Hypervigilance | Constant state of threat assessment; inability to relax; over-responsive to triggers | High | Health/Medical | High |
| Identity Documentation Loss | No identity papers; name confirmation uncertain; no birth records | High | Documents/Asylum | High |
| Grief and Loss | Death of father; loss of childhood; multiple losses unprocessed | High | Health/Medical | High |
| Emotional Dysregulation | Extreme anxiety, rage, withdrawal; mood swings; inability to regulate emotions | High | Health/Medical | High |
| Medical Complications | Injuries requiring treatment; infections; developmental delays | High | Health/Medical | Critical |
| Family Reunion Readiness | Mother also traumatized; reunion must be carefully managed to avoid re-trauma | High | Family/Reunion | High |
