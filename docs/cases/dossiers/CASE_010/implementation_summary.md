## Lessons Learned


1. **Multiple Vulnerabilities Interact and Compound**
   - Amara's case demonstrates how trauma, family conflict, health crisis, and poverty are not separate problems but deeply interconnected. The father's death created unresolved grief that made Amara vulnerable to family stress; the family conflict removed the grandmother's support exactly when the health crisis required maximum resources; the economic desperation made medical care inaccessible, worsening health; and poor health prevented school attendance, creating educational vulnerability and further isolation. Interventions must address the full system, not just isolated issues.

2. **Timing of Crisis Intervention is Critical**
   - Had intervention occurred in 2024 (during the early grief responses or when family conflict began), many of the severe consequences might have been prevented. By the time the case was opened in January 2026, Amara had suffered nearly 18 months of untreated trauma, months of untreated TB, and months of missed education. Earlier identification of risk factors—behavioral changes at school, family relationship breakdown—could have enabled prevention-focused intervention.
