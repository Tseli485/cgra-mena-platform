## Case Study 16: Teen Behavioral Crisis (Age 15)

**Scenario:** A 15-year-old boy is acting out violently at school, refusing to attend classes, and threatening harm to himself. Parents are overwhelmed and asking for immediate intervention.

**Background:**
- Parents separated 2 years ago
- Lives with mother, irregular visits with father
- Recent school bullying incident (unreported to authorities)
- Access to peer group with substance use risk

**Key Issues:**
- Behavioral health crisis (suicide risk assessment needed)
- School refusal (educational rights violation)
- Family conflict escalation
- Potential intervention coordination needed

**Tuteur Actions:**
1. Assess immediate safety (suicide/self-harm risk)
2. Coordinate with school counselor
3. Facilitate mental health evaluation
4. Document family situation thoroughly
5. Consider emergency protective measures if needed
6. Create behavior management plan with parents

**Legal Framework:**
- Mental health evaluation requirements
- School intervention protocols
- Parental consent for mental health treatment
- Age-appropriate involvement in decisions

**Lessons Learned:**
- Early identification of bullying prevents escalation
- Regular mental health check-ins important for teens
- Coordinated school-family intervention essential
- Peer support can be protective factor

**Resources Needed:**
- Mental health professional (immediate)
- School counselor coordination
- Crisis hotline (suicide prevention)
- Family therapist

## Case Study 17: Trafficking Victim Identification (Age 14)

**Scenario:** A tuteur visits a family and notices a 14-year-old girl showing signs of trafficking: recent unexplained absences from school, secretive phone use, new expensive belongings, behavioral changes, signs of physical abuse.

**Background:**
- Single parent household (economic stress)
- Recent housing instability
- Limited parental supervision
- Online relationships with unknown adults

**Key Issues:**
- Possible human trafficking
- Child sexual exploitation
- Evidence gathering for legal case
- Urgent intervention needed

**Tuteur Actions:**
1. Observe carefully without alerting traffickers
2. Create safe opportunity to speak privately
3. Ask open, non-judgmental questions
4. Document observations precisely
5. Report to authorities (mandatory reporting)
6. Coordinate with specialized anti-trafficking organization
7. Ensure immediate safety measures

**Legal Framework:**
- Mandatory trafficking reporting
- Evidence preservation
- Child victim protection laws
- Psychological evaluation protocols

**Lessons Learned:**
- Trafficking signs are subtle and vary by case
- Interviewing technique critical (non-traumatizing)
- Emergency intervention may save child's life
- Specialized resources necessary (not general family service)

**Resources Needed:**
- Anti-trafficking hotline (specialized)
- Law enforcement (trained unit)
- Victim advocate/support
- Safe housing if needed

## Case Study 18: LGBTQ+ Youth Identity Crisis (Age 16)

**Scenario:** A 16-year-old comes out as non-binary to tuteur, asking for help telling family. Expresses fear of rejection and homelessness. Family background includes religious conservatism and previous negative comments about LGBTQ+ people.

**Background:**
- Conservative religious family
- First time disclosing to adult
- Experiencing depression/anxiety
- Struggled with identity for 3+ years

**Key Issues:**
- Family rejection risk
- Mental health support needed
- Identity affirmation vs. family pressure
- Homelessness prevention

**Tuteur Actions:**
1. Validate identity and experiences
2. Assess family safety and rejection risk
3. Provide mental health resources
4. Help plan disclosure conversation
5. Prepare contingency (emergency housing options)
6. Connect to LGBTQ+ support groups
7. Monitor mental health closely

**Legal Framework:**
- Youth discrimination protections
- Mental health confidentiality
- Housing protections
- Anti-bullying school policies

**Lessons Learned:**
- LGBTQ+ youth have higher depression/suicide risk
- Family acceptance is protective factor
- Professional LGBTQ+-affirming counseling important
- Community support groups valuable

**Resources Needed:**
- LGBTQ+-affirming therapist
- Youth support groups (LGBTQ+)
- Crisis hotline (suicide prevention)
- Safe housing options if needed

## Case Study 19: Refugee Family Cultural Adjustment (Age 13)

**Scenario:** A 13-year-old from Syria arrived in Belgium 3 months ago. Struggling with language, lost friends in home country, experiencing nightmares and anxiety. Father depressed and unemployed. Mother trying to rebuild but overwhelmed.

**Background:**
- Experienced war trauma (family lost home)
- Language barrier (limited French)
- Social isolation (no peer friendships yet)
- Family financial stress (irregular housing)

**Key Issues:**
- PTSD/trauma symptoms (nightmares, hypervigilance)
- Language and educational access
- Social isolation and loneliness
- Family economic crisis

**Tuteur Actions:**
1. Trauma-informed assessment
2. Coordinate with school for language support
3. Connect to refugee services (housing, employment)
4. Refer to trauma-informed therapy
5. Help identify peer support opportunities
6. Advocate for family services access
7. Document family strengths and resilience

**Legal Framework:**
- Refugee family rights and services
- Education access for non-citizen children
- Healthcare access
- Labor standards protection

**Lessons Learned:**
- Trauma affects family functioning (not just individual)
- Language access critical for integration
- Peer relationships protective for adolescents
- Family employment/stability foundational

**Resources Needed:**
- Refugee integration services
- Trauma-informed therapist
- Language classes (school-based)
- Employment support for parents

## Case Study 20: Disability + Family Crisis (Age 12)

**Scenario:** A 12-year-old with cerebral palsy (mild, affects mobility) has a parent with terminal illness. Family is struggling with medical needs, emotional impact, and practical support. Child expressing guilt and anxiety about parent's death.

**Background:**
- Parent diagnosed 6 months ago (prognosis 1-2 years)
- Family previously stable and functional
- Other parent working but overwhelmed
- Child already experiencing stress from disability

**Key Issues:**
- Anticipatory grief and bereavement
- Caregiver burden (child may become informal caregiver)
- Disability + family crisis intersection
- Mental health impact on child

**Tuteur Actions:**
1. Normalize grief and emotions
2. Coordinate with hospice and medical team
3. Identify respite care/support services
4. Connect to bereavement counseling
5. Ensure child not expected to provide physical care
6. Monitor for depression/crisis
7. Plan for post-death arrangements and guardianship if needed

**Legal Framework:**
- Guardianship provisions if parent dies
- End-of-life decision-making
- Inheritance and financial planning
- Bereavement support services access

**Lessons Learned:**
- Crisis coordination is essential (medical + legal + emotional)
- Children should not be caregivers (even disabled children)
- Bereavement support improves outcomes
- Guardianship planning prevents crisis if parent dies

**Resources Needed:**
- Hospice services (if applicable)
- Disability support services
- Bereavement counselor
- Legal services (guardianship planning)
