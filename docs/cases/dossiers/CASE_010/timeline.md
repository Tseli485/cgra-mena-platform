# Timeline - Mixed Vulnerabilities 1 - Trauma, Family Conflict & Health Crisis


**2023 (September)**
- Father killed during community violence; Amara witnesses aftermath

**2024 (January-December)**
- Amara shows delayed grief responses; behavioral changes at school
- Father's family begins property dispute over his inheritance
- Grandmother provides increasing financial and emotional support to household
- Amara's school performance remains stable but social withdrawal begins

**2025 (August)**
- Grandmother's family confronts Marie over property; accusations and anger escalate
- Grandmother withdraws financial support (reduction of ~$15/month, critical for family)
- Conflict becomes public; extended family largely sides with grandmother
- Household isolation increases; Marie becomes increasingly stressed

**2025 (November)**
- Amara develops persistent cough and fever
- Marie delays medical visit due to cost concerns and family stress
- School reports 60% absenteeism

**2025 (December)**
- Amara examined at clinic; TB diagnosis confirmed (sputum-smear positive)
- Treatment protocol begins: 2-month intensive phase, 4-month continuation phase
- School attendance drops to 40%
- Behavioral outbursts and nightmares increase; one incident of physical punishment by mother

**2026 (January 15)**
- School social worker files child protection report
- Mme. Justine Mukanda assigned as tuteur; case opened

**2026 (January-February)**
- Initial assessment and safety planning
- Referral to TB clinic for supervised treatment
- Family mediation process begins with extended family
- Psychosocial assessment completed; trauma symptoms documented

**2026 (March)**
- Nutritional support program initiated (supplemental food package)
- Trauma-informed counseling begins with child psychologist
- School re-engagement plan developed

**2026 (April)**
- Family mediation yields partial property settlement
- Grandmother resumes limited financial support
- Sibling assessment completed; both siblings showing signs of stress

**2026 (May)**
- Amara completes intensive TB treatment phase
- School attendance improves to 70%
- Behavioral improvement noted; reduced nightmares
- Economic assessment: family eligible for emergency assistance grant

**2026 (June)**
- Transition to continuation TB phase (outpatient management)
- Grant disbursement approved: $200 for medical, nutrition, school supplies
- Amara returns to school full-time
- Psychological resilience building continues

**2026 (August)**
- TB treatment on track for completion (August/September 2026)
- School performance improving; catches up on missed coursework
- Family relationships stabilizing; reduced conflict
- Tuteur reports readiness for case transition planning
