# Legal Analysis - Family Conflict and Child Welfare - Afghan Minor

## Legal Framework Applied


| Law/Regulation | How It Applies | Responsible Party | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC, 1989) | Zahra has right to family unity; separation only if necessary for protection | Social Services + Court | Implemented |
| Belgian Child Protection Law | Mandates intervention when child welfare at risk; prioritizes family reunification | Child Protection Services | Implemented |
| EU Directive on Family Reunification (2003/86/EC) | Establishes rights for refugee families to live together | Immigration + Social Services | Applied |
| Mental Health Treatment Access Laws | Refugees entitled to psychological services regardless of status | Health Ministry | Implemented |
| Education Access Rights | All children have right to free education despite family status changes | Ministry of Education | Implemented |
| Foster Care Regulations | Establishes minimum standards for foster placements; oversight requirements | Social Services + Court | Implemented |
| Refugee Convention (1951) | Family unity principle applies to recognized refugees | Government Services | Applied |
| Data Protection/Family Privacy | Records of family services protected; privacy maintained | Social Services | Implemented |

| Problem | Description | Severity | Domain | Urgency |

| Child-Parent Separation | Zahra placed in foster care; emotional trauma from separation | High | Family/Reunion | Critical |

| Unresolved Grief | Death of mother unprocessed; compounded by refugee trauma | High | Health/Medical | High |
| Lack of Professional Mental Health Care | Neither father nor daughter has formal psychological support initially | High | Health/Medical | Critical |
| Social Isolation | No extended family in country; limited community connections | Medium | Family/Reunion | Medium |
| Financial Stress | Refugee stipend insufficient; father unable to work full-time | High | Finances/Banking | High |
