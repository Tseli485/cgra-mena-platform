# Timeline - Mixed Vulnerabilities 2 - Trafficking, Psychological Trauma & Educational Delay


**2018 (March)**
- Oumou born in Conakry, Guinea; father's identity unknown
- Lives with mother Aïssatou; extended family provides occasional support

**2020-2021 (Age 2-3)**
- Aïssatou's mental health declines; increasingly unable to work or care for Oumou
- Oumou periodically stays with maternal grandmother; inconsistent care arrangement

**2022 (April, Age 4)**
- Aïssatou's mental illness becomes severe; first hospitalization (2 weeks)
- Oumou's grandmother becomes primary caregiver
- Relatives discuss permanent placement; limited resources and space in extended family

**2023 (June, Age 5)**
- Mama Awa approaches Aïssatou at market with trafficking proposition
- Aïssatou agrees to arrangement; Oumou taken to Kinshasa under false pretenses
- Oumou placed in private household; confined, no communication with mother

**2023 (July-August)**
- Oumou experiences severe adjustment trauma; beaten for crying, resistance
- Begins working 14-16 hours daily in domestic labor
- Selective mutism develops; communication limited to whispers
- School enrollment promised but never occurs

**2023-2025 (Trafficking Period)**
- Continuous physical labor; malnutrition becomes severe
- Repeated beatings for infractions; injuries accumulate
- Psychological symptoms worsen: nightmares, dissociation, extreme fear responses
- No contact with mother; Aïssatou unaware of true situation
- Other girls in household provide limited peer support but also trauma-informed resilience

**2024 (Age 6)**
- Oumou's height and weight growth stall; physical deterioration evident
- Developmental milestones increasingly delayed; not walking normally; joint pain evident
- Emotional withdrawal increases; stops attempting communication
- Immunity weakens; recurring infections untreated

**2025 (June-July)**
- Neighbor observes concerning activity; reports to local authorities
- Police investigation initiated; planning for safe recovery
- Oumou's mother Aïssatou contacted; located after extended search
- Aïssatou in state of mental health crisis; hospitalized for acute episode

**2025 (August 15)**
- Police raid household; six girls recovered including Oumou
- Oumou transferred to emergency medical facility; medical assessment conducted
- Severe malnutrition, dehydration, multiple injuries, signs of abuse documented
- Psychological assessment indicates severe PTSD and developmental delay
- Initial trauma counseling and safety support provided

**2025 (August 20)**
- Oumou transferred to rehabilitation center for trafficking survivors
- Monsieur Amadou Diallo assigned as tuteur
- Initial case assessment conducted; comprehensive needs identified
- Contact with mother Aïssatou initiated; family reunification assessment begun

**2025 (September-October)**
- Intensive medical care: nutritional rehabilitation, wound treatment, infection management
- Psychological assessment confirms PTSD, developmental trauma, and selective mutism
- Educational assessment reveals profound gaps; language development delayed
- Mother Aïssatou's mental health status evaluated; treatment plan developed
- Initial visits between Oumou and mother (supervised, carefully paced)

**2025 (November-December)**
- Nutrition and health stabilization continuing; weight gain, infection clearance
- Trauma counseling begins; play-based therapy with trauma-informed therapist
- Educational assessment completed; accelerated learning plan developed
- Mother continues mental health treatment; capacity for childcare assessed
- Gradual increase in mother-child contact; relationship rebuilding begins

**2026 (January-February)**
- Oumou enters educational program (non-formal initially; 2 hours/day)
- Language development support intensive; significant progress
- Trauma symptoms gradually decreasing; selective mutism reducing; verbal communication increasing
- Mother Aïssatou stabilized on mental health medication; engaged in parenting support program

**2026 (March-April)**
- Oumou transitioned to part-time formal school enrollment (Grade 1 with additional support)
- Reading and numeracy instruction accelerated; progress monitored carefully
- Trauma recovery continuing; behavioral improvements noted
- Re-trafficking risk assessment and protective planning ongoing
- Family reunification plan finalized; phased transition to mother's care

**2026 (May-June)**
- Oumou remains in school; attendance and engagement strong
- Transition to living with mother (full-time) planned for June
- Mother's mental health stable; housing and economic support in place
- Tuteur shifts to monitoring phase; weekly visits continuing
