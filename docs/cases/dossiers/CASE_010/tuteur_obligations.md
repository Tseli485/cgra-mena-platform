# Tuteur Obligations - Mixed Vulnerabilities 1 - Trauma, Family Conflict & Health Crisis

## Step-by-Step Tuteur Actions


Mme. Justine Mukanda's responsibilities as tuteur for Amara K.:

1. **Case Assessment and Planning**
   - Conduct comprehensive initial assessment across all life domains (health, education, family, psychological, economic)
   - Develop written case plan with goals, timelines, and responsible parties
   - Review and update case plan every 3 months based on progress

2. **Medical Case Management**
   - Ensure TB treatment initiation and monitor medication adherence
   - Coordinate with health facility for clinic visits and follow-up appointments
   - Verify sputum test results at prescribed intervals (2, 5, 8 months)
   - Arrange transportation or financial support for medical visits as needed
   - Track nutritional status and ensure supplementary food delivery

3. **Psychosocial Support Coordination**
   - Identify and refer to qualified child psychologist or trauma counselor
   - Ensure counseling sessions occur as scheduled; address barriers if they don't
   - Communicate regularly with counselor regarding progress and any concerns
   - Support mother's participation in counseling and parenting education

4. **Family Strengthening and Mediation**
   - Facilitate communication between immediate family and extended family
   - Work with mediator to address family conflict; support implementation of agreements
   - Monitor family relationships and safety dynamics
   - Intervene if family conflict escalates or threatens child's safety

5. **Educational Advocacy and Support**
   - Verify school enrollment and regular attendance
   - Communicate with school regarding child's needs and progress
   - Ensure academic accommodations are in place during medical treatment
   - Support catch-up tutoring; monitor educational progress

6. **Economic Support Access**
   - Assess household economic situation and identify available assistance programs
   - Assist with applications for emergency grants, social assistance, or other programs
   - Follow up on application status; provide documentation support
   - Explore additional income-generating opportunities for mother

7. **Safety Monitoring and Protection**
   - Conduct regular home visits to assess living conditions and safety
   - Monitor for signs of abuse, neglect, or exploitation
   - Ensure adequate supervision, nutrition, and protection of all children in household
   - Take immediate protective action if safety is threatened; coordinate with child protection services

8. **Service Coordination and Navigation**
   - Maintain active contact with all service providers (health, school, counselor, etc.)
   - Hold multi-disciplinary case conferences to ensure integrated approach
   - Resolve service gaps or conflicts between providers
   - Ensure smooth transitions between service phases (e.g., intensive TB phase to continuation phase)

9. **Capacity Building for Primary Caregiver**
   - Support mother's participation in parenting education and stress management
   - Provide information, resources, and practical guidance on child health, development, and behavior
   - Build mother's confidence and capability to manage medical treatment and family dynamics
   - Recognize and reinforce mother's strengths and progress

10. **Documentation and Accountability**
    - Maintain detailed case file with all documents, assessments, and communications
    - Document all case activities, visits, and contacts with dates and summaries
    - Track case indicators and outcomes; provide regular updates
    - Ensure confidentiality and secure file management

11. **Advocacy and Rights Protection**
    - Ensure child's voices and preferences are heard in decision-making
    - Advocate for child's rights to health, education, and protection from violence
    - Challenge discriminatory practices or denial of services
    - Support legal processes related to guardianship, inheritance, or protection orders if needed

12. **Case Closure Planning and Sustainability**
    - By month 6, work with family and service providers to plan for transition
    - Ensure sustainable systems are in place for ongoing medical, educational, and psychosocial support
    - Build family's capacity to manage independently; reduce dependency on tuteur
    - Develop plan for monitoring after case closure; establish clear indicators for re-opening if needed
