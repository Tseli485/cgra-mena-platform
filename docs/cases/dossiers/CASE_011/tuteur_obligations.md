# Tuteur Obligations - Mixed Vulnerabilities 2 - Trafficking, Psychological Trauma & Educational Delay

## Step-by-Step Tuteur Actions


Monsieur Amadou Diallo's responsibilities as tuteur for Oumou D.:

1. **Comprehensive Initial Assessment and Care Planning**
   - Assess trafficking experience and trauma impacts across all life domains
   - Document physical health status, psychological condition, educational level
   - Evaluate family relationships and reintegration feasibility
   - Identify mother's mental health status and caregiving capacity
   - Assess re-trafficking risk and protection needs
   - Develop written case plan with goals for recovery and reintegration

2. **Medical Care Coordination and Health Monitoring**
   - Ensure emergency medical assessment and treatment
   - Oversee nutritional rehabilitation; monitor weight gain and health indicators
   - Ensure completion of vaccinations and preventive health care
   - Arrange regular medical check-ups; coordinate with health providers
   - Track infectious diseases, injuries, and health complications
   - Provide information on health, nutrition, and care to mother
   - Escalate medical concerns; ensure prompt treatment

3. **Psychological Trauma Support and Mental Health Coordination**
   - Refer to qualified trauma-informed child psychologist
   - Ensure therapeutic sessions occur as scheduled
   - Monitor psychological progress and trauma symptom reduction
   - Communicate regularly with therapist regarding goals and progress
   - Support mother's participation in family/couple trauma therapy
   - Provide emergency mental health support if crisis occurs
   - Document psychological progress and recommendations

4. **Educational Assessment, Enrollment, and Academic Support**
   - Conduct educational assessment; identify learning gaps and needs
   - Develop individualized education plan with clear learning goals
   - Identify appropriate educational program (non-formal, formal, hybrid)
   - Ensure school enrollment and attendance
   - Coordinate with school regarding trauma-informed support and accommodations
   - Ensure additional resource support (tutoring, literacy specialists)
   - Monitor academic progress; adjust plan as needed
   - Advocate for flexible, supportive approach given trauma and learning gaps

5. **Family Relationship Building and Reunification Support**
   - Assess family relationships and reintegration feasibility
   - Prepare Oumou psychologically for reunion; set realistic expectations
   - Prepare mother for reunion; provide psychoeducation and support
   - Facilitate initial supervised visits; provide emotional support
   - Gradually increase contact and move toward full-time reunification
   - Support attachment building and trust development
   - Provide mother-child counseling and relationship support
   - Monitor safety and wellbeing during and after reunification

6. **Maternal Mental Health and Caregiver Capacity Support**
   - Assess mother's mental health status and treatment needs
   - Ensure psychiatric evaluation and treatment initiation
   - Monitor medication adherence and symptom management
   - Support mother's engagement with mental health services
   - Provide psychoeducation regarding mental illness and parenting
   - Support mother's self-care and stress management
   - Connect mother to peer support and social networks
   - Recognize mother's challenges while ensuring child's safety

7. **Economic Support and Housing Stability**
   - Assess household economic situation and vulnerabilities
   - Identify available assistance programs (government, NGO, emergency)
   - Assist with applications and documentation; follow up on status
   - Ensure safe, adequate housing; arrange support for housing transitions
   - Explore income-generating opportunities for mother
   - Monitor economic stability; provide ongoing support coordination
   - Link family to long-term economic support systems

8. **Re-trafficking Prevention and Risk Management**
   - Conduct re-trafficking risk assessment; update periodically
   - Educate mother and Oumou on trafficking risks and warning signs
   - Develop protective plans addressing vulnerability factors
   - Monitor Oumou's relationships, activities, and safety
   - Train community members on protection needs
   - Maintain communication with local authorities
   - Take immediate protective action if trafficking concerns arise

9. **Service Coordination and Multi-Sectoral Collaboration**
   - Establish contact with all service providers (medical, psychological, educational, social)
   - Facilitate information-sharing and coordination (with confidentiality)
   - Hold multi-disciplinary case conferences at regular intervals
   - Address service gaps or conflicts between providers
   - Ensure integrated approach across all domains of support
   - Maintain comprehensive case documentation and communication

10. **Safety Monitoring and Protection**
    - Conduct regular home visits; assess living conditions and safety
    - Monitor Oumou's physical and emotional wellbeing
    - Assess mother's capacity to provide adequate supervision and protection
    - Identify safety risks; implement protective measures
    - Ensure appropriate childcare arrangements if mother working
    - Monitor for any signs of exploitation, abuse, or re-trafficking
    - Take action if safety is threatened; escalate to authorities as needed

11. **Documentation and Legal Support**
    - Verify Oumou's birth registration and legal documentation
    - Support legal guardianship and custody documentation
    - Ensure trafficking incident is documented and reported
    - Participate in legal processes if perpetrators are prosecuted
    - Maintain confidential case file with all assessments and documents
    - Ensure secure, confidential record-keeping

12. **Empowerment and Skill Development**
    - Support Oumou's voice and preferences in decision-making
    - Build Oumou's confidence and resilience
    - Support development of coping skills and self-care
    - Celebrate progress and achievements
    - Build mother's confidence and parenting capacity
    - Empower family toward independence and self-sufficiency

13. **Monitoring, Outcomes, and Transition**
    - Track indicators across health, psychological, educational, family, economic domains
    - Monitor trauma symptom reduction and resilience building
    - Document educational progress and catch-up
    - Monitor family stability and attachment
    - Evaluate re-trafficking risk reduction
    - Plan for case transition (from intensive to monitoring phase)
    - Establish monitoring plan after formal case closure
    - Celebrate recovery and family reunification success

## Process Timeline


**2018 (March)**
- Oumou born in Conakry, Guinea; father's identity unknown
- Lives with mother Aïssatou; extended family provides occasional support

**2020-2021 (Age 2-3)**
- Aïssatou's mental health declines; increasingly unable to work or care for Oumou
- Oumou periodically stays with maternal grandmother; inconsistent care arrangement

**2022 (April, Age 4)**
- Aïssatou's mental illness becomes severe; first hospitalization (2 weeks)
- Oumou's grandmother becomes primary caregiver
- Relatives discuss permanent placement; limited resources and space in extended family

**2023 (June, Age 5)**
- Mama Awa approaches Aïssatou at market with trafficking proposition
- Aïssatou agrees to arrangement; Oumou taken to Kinshasa under false pretenses
- Oumou placed in private household; confined, no communication with mother

**2023 (July-August)**
- Oumou experiences severe adjustment trauma; beaten for crying, resistance
- Begins working 14-16 hours daily in domestic labor
- Selective mutism develops; communication limited to whispers
- School enrollment promised but never occurs

**2023-2025 (Trafficking Period)**
- Continuous physical labor; malnutrition becomes severe
- Repeated beatings for infractions; injuries accumulate
- Psychological symptoms worsen: nightmares, dissociation, extreme fear responses
- No contact with mother; Aïssatou unaware of true situation
- Other girls in household provide limited peer support but also trauma-informed resilience

**2024 (Age 6)**
- Oumou's height and weight growth stall; physical deterioration evident
- Developmental milestones increasingly delayed; not walking normally; joint pain evident
- Emotional withdrawal increases; stops attempting communication
- Immunity weakens; recurring infections untreated

**2025 (June-July)**
- Neighbor observes concerning activity; reports to local authorities
- Police investigation initiated; planning for safe recovery
- Oumou's mother Aïssatou contacted; located after extended search
- Aïssatou in state of mental health crisis; hospitalized for acute episode

**2025 (August 15)**
- Police raid household; six girls recovered including Oumou
- Oumou transferred to emergency medical facility; medical assessment conducted
- Severe malnutrition, dehydration, multiple injuries, signs of abuse documented
- Psychological assessment indicates severe PTSD and developmental delay
- Initial trauma counseling and safety support provided

**2025 (August 20)**
- Oumou transferred to rehabilitation center for trafficking survivors
- Monsieur Amadou Diallo assigned as tuteur
- Initial case assessment conducted; comprehensive needs identified
- Contact with mother Aïssatou initiated; family reunification assessment begun

**2025 (September-October)**
- Intensive medical care: nutritional rehabilitation, wound treatment, infection management
- Psychological assessment confirms PTSD, developmental trauma, and selective mutism
- Educational assessment reveals profound gaps; language development delayed
- Mother Aïssatou's mental health status evaluated; treatment plan developed
- Initial visits between Oumou and mother (supervised, carefully paced)

**2025 (November-December)**
- Nutrition and health stabilization continuing; weight gain, infection clearance
- Trauma counseling begins; play-based therapy with trauma-informed therapist
- Educational assessment completed; accelerated learning plan developed
- Mother continues mental health treatment; capacity for childcare assessed
- Gradual increase in mother-child contact; relationship rebuilding begins

**2026 (January-February)**
- Oumou enters educational program (non-formal initially; 2 hours/day)
- Language development support intensive; significant progress
- Trauma symptoms gradually decreasing; selective mutism reducing; verbal communication increasing
- Mother Aïssatou stabilized on mental health medication; engaged in parenting support program

**2026 (March-April)**
- Oumou transitioned to part-time formal school enrollment (Grade 1 with additional support)
- Reading and numeracy instruction accelerated; progress monitored carefully
- Trauma recovery continuing; behavioral improvements noted
- Re-trafficking risk assessment and protective planning ongoing
- Family reunification plan finalized; phased transition to mother's care

**2026 (May-June)**
- Oumou remains in school; attendance and engagement strong
- Transition to living with mother (full-time) planned for June
- Mother's mental health stable; housing and economic support in place
- Tuteur shifts to monitoring phase; weekly visits continuing
