## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### General Questions

**Q: How long does it take to complete a phase?**
A: Each phase has a defined duration: Phase 1 (24 months), Phase 2 (48 months), Phase 3 (72 months), Phase 4 (72 months). Total: 18 years for complete lifecycle (0-18).

**Q: What if my child doesn't fit neatly into a phase?**
A: All children enter at their chronological age. If a child is 4 years old, they enter Phase 2 and follow that phase structure. Age ranges are flexible within phases to accommodate individual needs.

**Q: Can we move between phases early or late?**
A: Phase transitions typically occur at age boundaries. Early transitions require Multi-Disciplinary Team approval and comprehensive assessment. Extensions are allowed with documented justification.

### Financial Questions

**Q: How much will guardianship cost me?**
A: After government subsidy (€1,200-2,200/month), tuteur responsibility is €300-2,850/month depending on age and needs. See TABLE 7 (OBLIGATIONS_SUMMARY) for lifetime projections.

**Q: What if I can't afford all expenses?**
A: Government subsidies cover 60-70% of costs. TABLE 3 (OBLIGATIONS_SUMMARY) lists emergency assistance programs. Discuss financial hardship with your social worker.

**Q: Where do child benefits go?**
A: Opened in child's name at qualified bank (see financial_institutions.md). Guardian manages account per fiduciary duty detailed in FINANCES_OVERVIEW.md § 6.

**Q: What happens to the account at age 18?**
A: Account automatically converts to adult product. All funds belong to young adult. Gradual removal of guardian controls. See bank-specific transition procedures in financial_institutions.md.

### Obligations & Compliance

**Q: What happens if I miss a deadline?**
A: See OBLIGATIONS_SUMMARY TABLE 1 for deadline severity. URGENT items (medical screening, legal documentation) require immediate action. Alert social worker if delay is unavoidable.

**Q: How often do I need to report?**
A: Monthly case file updates minimum. Quarterly formal reports. Annual comprehensive assessment. See OBLIGATIONS_SUMMARY TABLE 4 for specific schedules.

**Q: Who decides which services the child receives?**
A: Multi-Disciplinary Team makes recommendations. Tuteur arranges services. Government authorities provide funding and oversight. See OBLIGATIONS_SUMMARY TABLE 5 for role clarity.

### School & Education

**Q: When should I enroll the child in school?**
A: Phase 2 (ages 2-6): Kindergarten enrollment by age 2-3. Phase 3 (ages 6-12): Primary school by age 6. Phase 4 (ages 12-18): Secondary school by age 12. See CHECKLIST_BY_AGE for specific timelines.

**Q: What if the child has language barriers?**
A: Language assessment in Phase 2 (CHECKLIST_BY_AGE Month 24.1). Support services required if delays identified. Budget €50-150/session for speech therapy (OBLIGATIONS_SUMMARY TABLE 3).

**Q: Can I choose the school?**
A: Yes, but enrollment must be confirmed by deadline shown in CHECKLIST_BY_AGE. School type (public/private) affects budgeting (see OBLIGATIONS_SUMMARY TABLE 2).

### Family Contact & Reunification

**Q: How often should I facilitate family contact?**
A: Minimum monthly contact required throughout all phases. If family is located, supervised visits arranged. Frequency increases ages 10+ (see CHECKLIST_BY_AGE FAMILY CONTACT sections).

**Q: How is reunification decided?**
A: Multi-Disciplinary Team assesses readiness (ages 6+, intensifies age 10+). See PHASES_FRAMEWORK § 3.3 and CHECKLIST_BY_AGE Month 6 (Phase 3).

**Q: What if reunification isn't possible?**
A: Alternative permanence plan developed (long-term guardianship, adoption consideration). Documented in care plan. Ongoing family contact may continue.

### Mental Health & Trauma

**Q: When should I arrange mental health services?**
A: Phase 1 (initial assessment by week 5). Phase 3 (formal evaluation within 3 months of school entry, see CHECKLIST_BY_AGE Week 11). Ongoing therapy recommended for all phases.

**Q: How do I know if the child has trauma?**
A: See PHASES_FRAMEWORK § 3 (Phase 3.1 discussion) for trauma screening. Psychologist assesses during initial evaluation. Trauma-informed care approaches detailed in professional training resources.

**Q: Will the child's mental health services continue?**
A: Yes, ongoing throughout all phases. Service provider changes between phases are normal. Ensure continuity by documenting progress and transferring records.

## Implementation Workflow

### Monthly Routine (All Tuteurs)

1. **Week 1 - Planning:** Review CHECKLIST_BY_AGE for current month items
2. **Week 2 - Administration:** Complete legal/financial/documentation tasks
3. **Week 3 - Appointments:** Attend school, health, and therapy appointments
4. **Week 4 - Reporting:** Update case file, prepare monthly summary

- Multi-disciplinary team meeting
- Progress report submission to authorities
- Financial accounting and budget review
- Special education/service assessments (if needed)

### Annual Routine (All Tuteurs)

- Comprehensive development assessment
- Care plan update and renewals
- Complete annual progress report
- Tax documentation for dependent deductions
- Benefit renewal and adjustment applications

## Success Indicators

By the end of each phase, you should have:

**Phase 1 (0-2 years):**
- [ ] Legal identity documentation secured
- [ ] Health baseline established and immunizations current
- [ ] Stable care placement with primary caregiver
- [ ] Family contact initiated or assessed

**Phase 2 (2-6 years):**
- [ ] School enrollment completed
- [ ] Language development assessed and supported
- [ ] Social skills developing appropriately
- [ ] Readiness for primary school confirmed

**Phase 3 (6-12 years):**
- [ ] Academic progress on track
- [ ] Mental health services established
- [ ] Family reunification plan completed or permanence confirmed
- [ ] Independent skill development begun

**Phase 4 (12-18 years):**
- [ ] Secondary education engagement strong
- [ ] Vocational interests explored
- [ ] Psychological support continued
- [ ] Life skills and independence preparation underway
